🌳What is Ecosia?

Ecosia is a Berlin-based search engine that uses its advertising profits to fund tree-planting operations around the world, having planted over 200 million trees since 2009.

Explain it like I'm 10

Ecosia is a search engine like Google, but when you search for something, the ads they show make money — and most of that money goes to planting trees! Just by searching the web normally, you help the planet.

They have a little counter that shows how many searches you have done and how many trees that helped plant. About 45 searches plants one tree!

Founded in 2009 by Christian Kroll in Berlin, Ecosia built its business model around a simple and compelling premise: use search advertising revenue to fund environmental restoration. The company uses Bing's search technology through a partnership with Microsoft, receiving a share of the advertising revenue generated from searches.

Ecosia is a certified B Corporation and a Social Purpose Company under German law, meaning its social and environmental mission is legally protected in its corporate structure. The company publishes monthly financial reports showing exactly how much money it received, how much it spent, and how much went to tree planting.

The company has planted over 200 million trees in more than 35 countries, focusing on areas with high biodiversity impact including Ethiopia, Brazil, Indonesia, Burkina Faso, and Spain. Each search costs Ecosia approximately 0.5 cents in revenue, meaning roughly 45 searches fund the planting of one tree.

⚙️How the Tree-Planting Works

Ecosia's tree planting is carefully managed and transparently reported.

Ecosia partners with tree-planting organizations and local communities in environmentally critical regions. Projects are selected based on biodiversity impact, carbon sequestration potential, and long-term sustainability. Ecosia funds not just planting but monitoring, ensuring trees survive and grow.

Monthly financial reports published on Ecosia's website show advertising revenue, operational costs, and tree-planting investments broken down by project. This radical transparency — unusual in the search industry — builds user trust and distinguishes Ecosia from companies making unverifiable environmental claims.

The company also operates a solar-powered data center and offsets the carbon costs of user searches. Ecosia claims to be not just carbon neutral but carbon negative — removing more CO2 from the atmosphere through its tree planting than its operations emit.

💰Ecosia as a Social Enterprise

Ecosia's corporate structure is designed to protect its mission forever.

As a Social Purpose Company and B Corp, Ecosia cannot be sold in a way that would compromise its environmental mission. Founder Christian Kroll has publicly refused acquisition offers, stating the company's purpose is more important than shareholder returns. This legally protected mission differentiates Ecosia from companies with ESG claims that could be abandoned.

Ecosia also owns its own solar power plants, generating renewable energy to power its servers and other operations. This vertical integration into clean energy ensures the company's environmental claims are backed by owned infrastructure rather than just renewable energy certificates.

The user counter showing personal search counts and tree contributions creates a powerful gamification element — users feel individual agency in environmental impact. This psychological mechanism has been key to Ecosia's organic growth through word-of-mouth, as users share their tree counts on social media.

📅History & Timeline

Ecosia's decade of green searching:

2009 — Founded

Christian Kroll founds Ecosia in Berlin after a world trip showed him the scale of deforestation — launches search engine with tree-planting mission.

2014 — B Corp Certified

Ecosia becomes a certified B Corporation, verifying that its social and environmental performance meets high standards.

2017 — Social Purpose Company

Ecosia restructures as a Social Purpose Company under German law, legally protecting the environmental mission from future investor pressure.
