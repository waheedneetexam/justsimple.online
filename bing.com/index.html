What is Bing?

Let's forget the technical stuff and use a simple story first.

🔍 Imagine asking a really smart robot friend...

You know how you can type a question into a search box and find answers? That's a search engine. Bing is Microsoft's version of that — kind of like Google, but made by the people who made Windows.

Bing is like a super-smart librarian who has read every single page on the internet. You ask it anything, and it tells you exactly where to find the answer — websites, images, videos, maps, news, you name it.

But here's the cool part: since February 2023, Bing added an AI robot friend called Copilot. Now instead of just giving you a list of links, Bing can actually talk to you, write essays for you, draw pictures for you, and have real conversations — like ChatGPT but built right into search!

So Bing is like Google but made by Microsoft, and it has an AI helper called Copilot that can write essays, draw pictures, and have real conversations with you. It's the #2 search engine in the world, used by hundreds of millions of people every day.

📅Timeline

Key milestones in Bing's journey from launch to AI search leader.

1998 — MSN Search

Microsoft enters the search game with MSN Search, powered by third-party results. Largely ignored while Google rose to dominance.

2006 — Windows Live Search

Rebranded and revamped, but still struggling to capture market share against Google's growing dominance.

June 2009 — Bing Launches

Microsoft rebrands as "Bing" and positions it as a "decision engine" — more than just a search engine. Backed by a massive $100M marketing campaign.

2010 — Yahoo Partnership

Yahoo switches its search backend to Bing in a landmark 10-year deal, dramatically increasing Bing's market share and query volume overnight.

2016 — LinkedIn Acquisition

Microsoft acquires LinkedIn for $26.2B. Professional data begins enriching Bing's knowledge graph and search capabilities.

February 2023 — Copilot Integration

Microsoft announces Bing with ChatGPT-powered Copilot — the biggest shake-up in search in two decades. Market share jumps from 2.81% to 5.01%.

2024–2025 — AI Search Expansion

Copilot gains 33M users. DALL-E 3 image generation added free. Deep integration with Microsoft 365 and Windows. Advertising revenue hits $11.59B.

✨Summary

🎯 The one-sentence version

Bing is Microsoft's search engine and AI platform — the #2 search engine globally, processing 900 million searches daily across 238 regions, now transformed by Copilot AI into a conversational search assistant that can write, create images, and hold real conversations.

While Google dominates with nearly 90% market share, Bing's AI-first bet with Copilot made 2023 the most disruptive year in search since Google's own launch — and the race is far from over.
