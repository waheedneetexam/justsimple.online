Zoom: How the Video Conferencing Giant Works

An independent deep-dive into Zoom's WebRTC architecture, freemium subscription model, $4.5B revenue engine, and its transformation from a video app into a full enterprise communications platform.

📹What is Zoom?

Zoom is the video conferencing tool that turned 'jumping on a call' into a universal phrase — used by 300M+ daily participants for everything from business meetings to family dinners.

Explain it like I'm 10

Zoom is a programme that lets you see and talk to people on your computer or phone, even if they're on the other side of the world. It became really famous during COVID when schools closed and kids had to attend class from home, and when people had to work from their houses instead of offices. You click a link, and suddenly you can see all your classmates or workmates on screen at the same time.

Zoom was better than older video calling tools because it was much simpler to use and worked well even when your internet wasn't very fast. The basic version is free, which is why schools and families used it. Businesses pay for the full version so they can have longer meetings and more features. Zoom became so popular so fast that 'Zooming someone' became a normal thing people say, like 'Googling' or 'Ubering'.

Zoom Video Communications was founded in 2011 by Eric Yuan, a former Cisco engineer who had led the development of Cisco WebEx. Yuan left Cisco after his proposals for a new, mobile-first video conferencing product were rejected, and founded Zoom with the goal of making video conferencing reliable and easy enough that people would actually use it without being forced to. He spent two years building the product with a team of engineers before launching in 2013 — a period he later described as the most important investment in getting the architecture right.

Zoom's growth before COVID was already impressive: it reached a $1B valuation (unicorn status) in 2017 and went public in April 2019 at a $9B valuation, one of the most successful tech IPOs of the year. But COVID-19 transformed the company. Daily meeting participants grew from 10 million in December 2019 to 300 million by April 2020 — a 30x increase in four months. The company's market cap peaked at $160B in October 2020, a 500% increase from its IPO price.

Post-pandemic, Zoom has navigated the difficult transition from hypergrowth back to normalised growth as in-person work resumed. Revenue stabilised around $4.5B annually as enterprise accounts matured. Zoom has responded by expanding its product portfolio beyond video calls to include Zoom Phone (cloud telephony), Zoom Contact Centre, Zoom AI Companion (AI meeting assistant), Zoom Docs, and Zoom Whiteboard — positioning itself as a full enterprise communications platform rather than a single-product video tool.

⚙️How Zoom Works

Zoom's video quality advantage comes from a carefully engineered stack built for low-bandwidth reliability, not just fast connections.

Zoom's key technical advantage over legacy conferencing tools is its multimedia routing architecture. Rather than sending all video streams peer-to-peer between participants (which degrades badly on poor connections), Zoom routes all media through its globally distributed Multimedia Routers (MMRs). This centralised routing allows Zoom to adapt video quality per participant based on their connection speed, mute individual streams, and perform server-side processing — all without requiring participants to have high-bandwidth connections.

Zoom's video codec uses a proprietary implementation optimised for screen sharing and talking heads — the two dominant video conferencing use cases. Screen sharing is compressed very efficiently because slides and documents have low entropy (many repeated pixels). Talking heads are prioritised by face detection algorithms to maintain sharpness where viewers look most. Zoom's echo cancellation, background noise suppression, and automatic gain control are implemented in software and have been refined over a decade of real-world usage data.

Zoom AI Companion (launched 2023) brings AI-generated meeting summaries, action item extraction, and real-time translation directly into the meeting experience. The AI features are powered by Zoom's own large language model trained on meeting transcripts and supplemented by third-party model APIs. AI Companion is included at no additional cost for paid subscribers — a competitive counter to Microsoft Teams Copilot (which charges $30/user/month extra) that positions Zoom as the value leader in AI-enhanced communications.

💰How Zoom Makes Money

Zoom's freemium model converts individual free users into enterprise accounts through a bottom-up adoption strategy.

Enterprise subscriptions are Zoom's primary revenue driver. The Enterprise tier (custom pricing) and Business tier ($219.90/year per user) provide unlimited meeting duration, large participant limits, cloud recording, and admin controls. Zoom has 220,000+ enterprise customers contributing the majority of its $4.5B revenue. Enterprise contracts are often multi-year, generating high revenue predictability and switching costs from deep IT integration.

Zoom's bottom-up adoption model is central to its sales efficiency. Individual employees discover Zoom through free personal use, bring it into their companies for work, and the company eventually purchases enterprise licences to gain admin control, compliance features, and volume pricing. This Product-Led Growth (PLG) approach meant Zoom could acquire enterprise customers with a much lower Customer Acquisition Cost than traditional enterprise software sold through direct sales forces — a key reason Zoom achieved better unit economics than legacy conferencing vendors.

Zoom Phone (cloud telephony to replace traditional desk phones) and Zoom Contact Centre (cloud call centre platform) are the two highest-growth revenue lines beyond core video. Zoom Phone has over 5 million seats and is growing as enterprises replace legacy PBX phone systems. The expansion from video into voice and contact centre triples Zoom's addressable market. Zoom's strategy is to make every business communication modality (video, voice, chat, webinar, events) flow through the Zoom platform.

✨What Made Zoom Win

Zoom's success is built on a combination of technical reliability and radical simplicity that competitors underestimated.

🔗

One-Click Joining

Anyone can join a Zoom meeting from a link without creating an account. This friction elimination was revolutionary — competitors required accounts and software installation.

🤖

AI Companion

Free AI meeting summaries, action items, and transcription included with all paid plans — no $30/user Copilot premium required like with Microsoft Teams.

📞

Zoom Phone

Cloud telephony replacing traditional desk phones. 5M+ seats make Zoom Phone the fastest-growing unified communications product in the market.

🌐

Global Infrastructure

17 data centres worldwide with intelligent routing ensure low latency for international calls. Works reliably in regions where competitors struggle.

📊

Product-Led Growth

Free personal users bring Zoom into enterprises organically. Lower sales costs than traditional enterprise software — a key driver of Zoom's profitability.

🔒

AES-256 Encryption

End-to-end encryption for meetings, zero-trust architecture, and HIPAA/FedRAMP compliance for healthcare and government customers.
