MSN: How Microsoft's News Portal Works

An independent deep-dive into MSN's content aggregation model, Microsoft Start personalisation system, Copilot AI integration, and how a 1995-era portal remains relevant with 500M+ monthly visitors.

🌐 msn.com

Editorial Independence: JustSimple.Online is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by MSN. All analysis is independent research for informational purposes only.

🗞️What is MSN?

MSN is Microsoft's news and content portal — the personalised homepage that 500M+ people use to read news, check weather, follow sports, and track finance, powered by Microsoft Start AI.

Explain it like I'm 10

MSN is a website that Microsoft made where you can see news, weather, sports scores, and other information all in one place. When people install Windows, MSN is often set as the default page when you open a browser. It's like a personalised newspaper that shows you news about topics you're interested in.

MSN makes money by showing adverts alongside the news articles. When you click through to read a full article, MSN makes a small amount from advertising. Microsoft also uses MSN to promote its other products like Bing search and Microsoft 365. It's not a particularly exciting product compared to Google News or Apple News, but it has a huge audience because it comes pre-installed with Windows on hundreds of millions of computers.

MSN (The Microsoft Network) launched in August 1995 alongside Windows 95 as an online service competing with AOL, CompuServe, and Prodigy. It was bundled with Windows 95 installation discs, instantly giving it tens of millions of potential users. Over the following decades, MSN evolved from a proprietary online service to an internet portal, then a content aggregation platform. MSN.com became the homepage default for Internet Explorer, maintaining massive traffic through Microsoft's OS and browser distribution.

MSN's modern incarnation as a content portal is powered by Microsoft Start (formerly MSN) — an AI-driven personalisation engine that curates news feeds based on reading history, location, and explicit interest selections. The platform aggregates content from 3,000+ news publishers, providing their articles to MSN's audience in exchange for traffic. MSN's editorial team curates and surfaces articles, while the Microsoft Start algorithm personalises which articles each user sees.

Microsoft integrated Copilot AI (powered by GPT-4) into MSN in 2023, adding AI-generated article summaries, a chat interface for news research, and AI-curated content discovery. This positions MSN as an AI-enhanced news experience that competitors without their own AI infrastructure cannot easily replicate. MSN's traffic advantage (pre-installation with Windows, Edge browser default) combined with Copilot AI gives Microsoft a path to defend the portal's relevance against pure-play news aggregators.

⚙️How MSN Works

MSN aggregates content from thousands of publishers and personalises it using Microsoft Start AI — delivered to hundreds of millions of Windows and Edge users by default.

Microsoft Start's personalisation engine analyses each user's reading patterns, location, and explicit interest selections (Sports? Technology? Business? Entertainment?) to build a personalised content feed. The algorithm weights recency, relevance, and engagement signals from millions of users to determine which of the 3,000+ publisher articles to surface to each person. Content from CNN, BBC, Reuters, AP, and thousands of regional publishers is aggregated and ranked for each user's feed.

MSN's publisher relationships operate on a traffic-sharing model. Publishers provide their article content (via RSS feeds or direct API integrations) in exchange for traffic referrals from MSN's massive audience. Unlike Google AMP or Facebook Instant Articles, MSN often hosts article content directly, allowing advertising to be served within the MSN environment rather than on the publisher's own site. This is a point of tension with publishers who prefer traffic to their own properties.

Copilot's integration into MSN allows users to ask questions about news articles and receive AI-generated summaries and analysis. For example, a user reading a complex geopolitical article can ask Copilot 'explain the background' and receive an AI-generated context summary. This AI layer differentiates MSN from pure news aggregators and creates a research-style reading experience that is harder for smaller portals to replicate.

💰How MSN Makes Money

MSN generates revenue through display advertising on its portal, with advertising revenue shared with publisher partners.

Display advertising is MSN's primary revenue source. With 500M+ monthly visitors spending time reading news, weather, sports, and finance content, MSN offers premium digital advertising inventory. Display, video, and native advertising from major consumer brands and news event advertisers generate the bulk of revenue. MSN's audience skews older than social media platforms — Windows default browser users across demographics — creating specific value for certain advertiser categories.

MSN operates a revenue-sharing model with publishers. When users read articles hosted on MSN, advertising revenue is split between Microsoft and the content publisher. This model incentivises publishers to contribute high-quality content while giving Microsoft a low-cost content acquisition strategy. However, it creates a fundamental tension: publishers want traffic to their own sites (where they capture 100% of advertising revenue), not to MSN-hosted versions.

MSN's strategic value to Microsoft extends beyond direct advertising revenue. MSN is the default new tab page for Microsoft Edge, which generates significant Bing search queries — directing users from news reading to Bing-powered search. Every Edge+MSN user represents a potential Bing search query, making MSN's 500M monthly visitors a significant source of Bing's search volume. This search referral value makes MSN worth maintaining even if its direct advertising margin is modest.

📅History & Timeline

From a Windows 95 launch feature to Microsoft's AI-enhanced news platform.

1995 — MSN Launches with Windows 95

The Microsoft Network launches as an AOL competitor, bundled with Windows 95. Millions of users are automatically on MSN via OS bundling.

1998 — MSN Pivots to Internet Portal

MSN transitions from proprietary online service to internet portal, competing with Yahoo!, Excite, and AltaVista.

2004 — MSN Spaces Blog Platform

MSN Spaces launches as a blog platform. Later becomes Windows Live Spaces, then discontinued in 2011.

2012 — MSN Rebrand and Modern Design

MSN relaunches with a cleaner, modern design focused on news aggregation and personalisation.

2023 — Copilot AI Integration

GPT-4 powered Copilot integrated into MSN and Edge new tab. AI summaries and chat added to news reading experience.

2024 — Microsoft Start Personalisation Advances

Microsoft Start AI personalisation capabilities expanded. MSN increasingly positions as an AI-curated information dashboard.
