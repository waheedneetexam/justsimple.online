🌎What is Infobae?

Infobae is Latin America's most-read Spanish-language digital news portal, delivering 24/7 breaking news, politics, economy, and culture coverage to readers across the Americas.

Explain it like I'm 10

Infobae is like a big news website that millions of people in South America and beyond read every day. It started in Argentina but now people all over Latin America use it to find out what is happening in the world.

Unlike old newspaper websites, Infobae was born on the internet so it updates its news all day and night, very fast, whenever something important happens.

Founded in 2002 by Daniel Hadad in Buenos Aires, Infobae was one of the first major Argentine news sources built entirely for digital distribution rather than adapting a print publication. This digital-native approach allowed it to innovate rapidly in content formats and distribution.

Infobae expanded beyond Argentina to launch regional editions for Mexico, Colombia, the United States (Spanish-language), and broader Latin America. This multi-market strategy has made it the most-read Spanish-language news portal in the region, surpassing traditional media organizations with much longer histories.

The site is known for its aggressive coverage pace, publishing hundreds of articles daily across politics, crime, economy, entertainment, health, and technology. Infobae's breaking news speed and willingness to cover controversial topics have made it a primary source for journalists and political observers across Latin America.

⚙️Multi-Country Digital Strategy

Infobae built a pan-Latin American presence by adapting its model to each market.

Each regional edition (Argentina, Mexico, Colombia, US) has a dedicated editorial team producing locally relevant content while sharing resources and infrastructure. This distributed editorial model allows Infobae to compete with local news sources in each market while benefiting from the broader network.

The US Spanish-language edition targets the large and growing US Hispanic community, a market traditionally underserved by major digital news platforms. With 60+ million Spanish speakers in the US, this represents a significant and undermonetized audience.

Video content has been a major investment area, with Infobae producing news videos, documentary series, and live coverage of major events. The video strategy supports both the website and strong social media distribution, particularly on YouTube and Facebook where Spanish-language news content generates high engagement.

💰Digital Advertising and Business

Infobae has built a sustainable digital media business in Latin America's challenging advertising market.

Infobae generates revenue primarily through digital advertising — programmatic display, video pre-roll, branded content, and sponsored sections. The scale of its audience across five markets makes it an efficient buy for advertisers seeking pan-Latin American reach.

Infobae has developed branded content capabilities, producing sponsored journalism, native advertising sections, and event coverage for major regional and multinational advertisers. This premium advertising formats generate higher CPMs than programmatic inventory.

The site's data infrastructure enables sophisticated audience targeting, allowing advertisers to reach specific demographic and interest segments across Infobae's properties — a significant advantage in markets where third-party data has historically been limited.

📅History & Timeline

Infobae's rise to Latin American digital media leadership:

2002 — Founded in Argentina

Daniel Hadad launches Infobae.com in Buenos Aires as a digital-native news site focused on Argentine politics and business.

2010 — Expansion Begins

Infobae begins expanding beyond Argentina, launching Mexico edition to serve the region's largest Spanish-speaking market.

2015 — 50M Monthly Users

Infobae surpasses 50 million monthly unique visitors, establishing itself as Latin America's most-read digital news site.

2018 — Colombia and US Editions

New editions launch for Colombia and US Spanish-speaking audiences, completing the core multi-market expansion strategy.

2023 — 100M Users

Infobae reaches 100 million monthly unique visitors, cementing its position as the leading Spanish-language digital news portal.
