What is Phoenix Wallet?

Let's forget the technical stuff and use a fun story.

⚡ Imagine sending money like sending a WhatsApp message...

You know how sending a text message to a friend anywhere in the world is instant and free? That's what Lightning Network is for Bitcoin. Instead of waiting 10 minutes and paying big fees, you can send Bitcoin in less than a second for almost nothing.

But using Lightning normally is complicated — it's like trying to set up Wi-Fi yourself instead of just plugging a cable in. Phoenix Wallet is the app that makes all of that complicated stuff invisible. You just download it, and it works — automatically.

The best part? Your Bitcoin is yours. It's saved with a special 12-word secret only you know. Not even the company that made Phoenix can touch your money. It's like a piggy bank that only you have the key to — but it can also send money anywhere in the world instantly. 🌍

The company that made it is called ACINQ — they are like the engineers who built the roads that the Lightning Network cars drive on. Phoenix is just their friendly GPS app for those roads.

🚀Key Features

Everything built into Phoenix — no add-ons required.

🔄

Auto Channel Management

Lightning channels open, resize, and manage themselves. You never touch a channel setting.

🌿

Taproot Channels

~20% cheaper on-chain ops. Channels look like regular Bitcoin transactions on-chain for privacy.

📋

BOLT12 Offers

Reusable, non-expiring payment codes — like a Bitcoin address but for Lightning.

👛

Multi-Wallet

Manage multiple wallets in one app — name them, add photos, set individual PINs.

🔐

12-Word Recovery

Lose your phone? Enter your 12-word seed in a fresh install and recover everything.

⛓️

Splice Out

Send Bitcoin directly to any on-chain address from your Lightning wallet — like a normal BTC wallet.

🔍

No KYC

Download and use with no identity verification whatsoever. Just install and go.

🖥️

phoenixd (Server)

Run Phoenix on a server — perfect for merchants, developers, and automated payment flows.

⚡

Zero-Conf Swaps

Deposits become usable immediately — before they even get a blockchain confirmation.

⚠️

US Regulatory Notice

Phoenix was removed from the US App Store on May 3, 2024, due to regulatory uncertainty about whether Lightning wallet providers could be classified as Money Services Businesses. The wallet continues operating normally worldwide outside the US.

📅History & Evolution

From ACINQ's early Lightning work to the wallet of the future.

2015 — ACINQ Founded

ACINQ formed as a Bitcoin technology company, focusing on Lightning Network research and infrastructure.

2017–2018 — Eclair Released

ACINQ releases Eclair, their open-source Lightning Network implementation, becoming one of the three major Lightning implementations.

2019 — Phoenix Wallet Launched

First version of Phoenix released — bringing automatic Lightning channel management to mobile users for the first time.

2023 — Splicing Update

Major architecture overhaul: one dynamic channel per wallet using splicing technology. Eliminated unpredictable fees and multiple UTXOs.

2023 — Swaproot Protocol

New on-chain deposit protocol using Taproot + MuSig2. 16–27% cheaper deposits, better privacy.

2024 — Taproot Channels + BOLT12

Taproot channels (~20% cost savings), BOLT12 offer support (reusable invoices), and multi-wallet feature added.

May 2024 — US App Store Removal

Phoenix withdrawn from US App Store due to regulatory uncertainty. Available worldwide outside US.

2025 — Ongoing

Continued UI improvements, phoenixd server development, and Lightning Network protocol advancements.
