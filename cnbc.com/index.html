📊What is CNBC?

CNBC is the world's leading business news network, broadcasting live market data, financial analysis, and economic news 24 hours a day to investors and business professionals globally.

Explain it like I'm 10

CNBC is a TV channel and website all about money and business. When the stock market opens, CNBC shows you every second whether stocks are going up or down, and why.

Business people watch CNBC to know what is happening with their money and companies. It is like a sports channel but for business and investing.

Launched in 1989 by NBC and Cablevision, CNBC quickly established itself as the go-to source for market coverage, particularly during the dot-com boom of the 1990s when stock market interest exploded among retail investors. The network's real-time ticker tapes and floor correspondents became iconic features of the financial media landscape.

CNBC operates a global network including CNBC Europe, CNBC Asia, and regional partnerships reaching over 400 million households in more than 100 countries. CNBC.com and the CNBC app extend the network's reach to digital audiences seeking market data, video content, and financial analysis.

Owned by NBCUniversal (a subsidiary of Comcast), CNBC benefits from integration with NBC News while maintaining a distinct financial focus. The network's morning show Squawk Box and afternoon programs like Fast Money and Mad Money with Jim Cramer have developed loyal audiences among active investors.

⚙️Live Market Coverage

CNBC's live market coverage during trading hours is its core differentiator.

During New York Stock Exchange and NASDAQ trading hours, CNBC provides wall-to-wall coverage with reporters on the trading floor, analyst commentary, and real-time data integration. The iconic stock ticker running across the bottom of the screen became a television staple that other networks have imitated.

CNBC's relationships with Wall Street analysts, fund managers, and corporate executives give the network regular access to market-moving information and commentary. Interviews on CNBC can move stock prices, giving the network significant influence beyond its audience size.

The network has invested heavily in data visualization and on-air graphics to help viewers understand complex financial information. Real-time charts, sector performance heatmaps, and interactive data displays make CNBC a visually distinctive news broadcast.

💰Digital Expansion

CNBC has successfully extended its television brand into digital platforms.

CNBC.com averages over 100 million monthly unique visitors, making it one of the most visited financial news websites globally. The site provides breaking news, market data, portfolio tools, and video content alongside original reporting from CNBC's journalist network.

CNBC Make It, a personal finance vertical targeting millennials and Gen Z, covers career advice, investing basics, and financial independence topics. This segment successfully extends the CNBC brand to younger audiences less engaged with traditional business television.

CNBC Pro is a premium subscription service offering ad-free content, advanced market analysis, investment recommendations from top analysts, and exclusive subscriber events. It represents CNBC's entry into the paid financial information market.

✨CNBC Programming and Features

CNBC offers distinctive programming formats that have built loyal viewer communities.

🌅

Squawk Box

CNBC's flagship morning program setting the agenda for market participants before US trading opens, with top executive interviews.

😡

Mad Money

Jim Cramer's energetic evening show offering stock picks and investment analysis — one of TV's most distinctive finance programs.

⚡

Fast Money

After-market trading desk show featuring professional traders analyzing the day's action and identifying the next day's opportunities.

📱

CNBC.com

Comprehensive financial news website with real-time market data, portfolio tracking, and video content from across the CNBC network.

📅History & Timeline

CNBC's history of financial journalism:

1989 — CNBC Founded

Consumer News and Business Channel launches as a joint venture between NBC and Cablevision, initially focusing on consumer and financial topics.

1991 — NBC Takes Control

NBC purchases full ownership of CNBC from Cablevision, integrating it into the NBC news operation.

1995 — Merges with FNN

CNBC acquires rival Financial News Network, eliminating its main competitor and solidifying its position in financial television.

2007 — Global Expansion

CNBC expands its international network, establishing regional channels in Europe and Asia with local programming and partnerships.

2013 — Digital Push

CNBC launches a major digital initiative, redesigning CNBC.com and investing in mobile apps to capture shifting media consumption patterns.
