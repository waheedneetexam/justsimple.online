📰What is Dzen?

Dzen is Russia's answer to a Medium-meets-YouTube-meets-news-aggregator — a personalised content platform that reaches the majority of Russia's internet users.

Explain it like I'm 10

Imagine an app that watches what kind of articles and videos you enjoy, then fills your screen with more things just like them. You might get cooking recipes, football news, funny stories, or science videos — all chosen just for you based on what you've read before. That's Dzen. It's like having a magazine that reshuffles itself every time you open it to show exactly what you want.

Dzen also lets ordinary people write their own articles and videos and share them with millions of readers. A doctor can write health advice, a traveller can post trip stories, a chef can share recipes — and Dzen's algorithm decides who sees them based on their interests. So both readers and creators benefit from the same smart recommendation system.

Dzen (Дзен) is a Russian content recommendation and publishing platform operated by VK Company Limited (formerly known as Mail.ru Group) following its spin-off from Yandex in 2022. Originally launched as "Yandex.Zen" in 2017, it was Yandex's attempt to build a personalised content feed similar to what Facebook News Feed or Google Discover provides in the West. The platform aggregates articles, blog posts, short videos, and podcasts from both professional media outlets and individual creators, serving them through a machine-learning-powered recommendation engine.

Dzen was spun off from Yandex in October 2022 as part of a broader restructuring of Yandex's businesses amid geopolitical pressures following Russia's invasion of Ukraine. The Zen and News products were sold to VK Company (the operator of Russia's largest social network VKontakte) for approximately 1 billion rubles, while Yandex retained its core search, maps, taxi, and cloud businesses. This transfer represented a significant reshaping of the Russian internet landscape.

Today Dzen reaches over 60 million monthly users in Russia, making it one of the country's most trafficked web properties. The platform serves as a critical distribution channel for Russian media publishers — from major news outlets like RBC and Kommersant to independent bloggers and niche content creators. Its short video feature, added in 2021, competes directly with TikTok and YouTube Shorts for Russian Gen-Z attention.

⚙️How Dzen's Recommendation Engine Works

Dzen's core technology is a personalised content feed built on years of Yandex's machine learning expertise in understanding Russian-language text and user behaviour.

Dzen's recommendation algorithm works by building a detailed interest profile for each user based on their reading behaviour, viewing time, scroll patterns, and explicit interactions (likes, saves, shares). The system uses collaborative filtering combined with content-based filtering — matching users with content that similar users enjoyed while also directly modelling the content's topics against the user's declared and inferred interests. Russian-language NLP capabilities inherited from Yandex's decade of search research give Dzen strong semantic understanding of article content.

Content creators on Dzen publish through a dedicated web editor. Articles are analysed immediately upon publication: the algorithm classifies the topic, assesses content quality signals (length, image quality, formatting, originality), and makes an initial distribution decision. New creators start with small test distributions to gauge early engagement; strong engagement signals trigger broader amplification. This cold-start handling is critical for making the platform viable for new creators who have no existing follower base.

The short video feed on Dzen uses computer vision to analyse video content alongside text descriptions, applying a separate recommendation model trained on video engagement signals: completion rate, replays, and shares. Given the intense competition from TikTok (blocked in Russia but still accessible via VPN) and VK Clips, Dzen's video product must compete on recommendation quality to retain users. The platform has invested in vertical video production tools and creator monetisation to incentivise Reels-style content.

💰How Does Dzen Make Money?

Dzen monetises through advertising placed within the content feed and revenue sharing with creators — a model that aligns platform and creator incentives.

Dzen's primary revenue model is native advertising inserted into personalised feeds. Promoted articles and sponsored videos are designed to match the format of organic content, making them less disruptive than traditional banner ads. Advertisers pay on a CPM or CPC basis, and Dzen's targeting capabilities — built on rich behavioural data from millions of daily users — allow precise audience segmentation by age, geography, interests, and device type.

Dzen operates a creator monetisation programme that shares advertising revenue with content creators based on the views and engagement their content generates. This revenue-sharing model incentivises creators to produce high-quality, engaging content, which in turn makes the feed more valuable to users and more attractive to advertisers — a classic platform flywheel. Creators with large audiences can earn meaningful income, ranging from small supplementary earnings for casual bloggers to significant monthly revenue for popular channels.

As part of the VK ecosystem since 2022, Dzen benefits from cross-platform integration with VKontakte, Mail.ru, and OK.ru (Odnoklassniki). This allows shared user accounts, unified advertising inventory, and content distribution across multiple Russian social platforms simultaneously. For advertisers, this creates a compelling one-stop Russian digital media buy; for creators, it offers multi-platform distribution from a single dashboard.

📅Dzen History

From a Yandex experiment to Russia's dominant content platform in under a decade.

2015 — Internal Project at Yandex

Yandex begins experimenting with personalised news feeds as a feature for Yandex Browser and Yandex mobile homepage.

2017 — Yandex.Zen Launches Publicly

Released as a standalone product; the recommendation engine and creator publishing tools go live for all users.

2019 — Creator Monetisation Launched

Revenue sharing for creators begins, triggering rapid growth in the number of content channels and content volume on the platform.

2021 — Short Video Added

Launches vertical short video format to compete with TikTok and Instagram Reels for Russian user attention.

2022 — Transferred to VK Company

As Yandex restructures amid geopolitical pressures, Zen and Yandex News are sold to VK Company and rebranded as simply "Dzen".

2023 — Full VK Integration

Deep integration with VKontakte and Mail.ru completed, creating unified advertising and content distribution across Russia's largest digital media group.
