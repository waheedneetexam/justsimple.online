🍕What is DoorDash?

DoorDash is the largest food delivery platform in the United States, connecting over 37 million customers with more than 700,000 restaurants and merchants for on-demand delivery.

Explain it like I'm 10

DoorDash is an app where you order food from restaurants and a delivery person brings it to your door. You can order from almost any restaurant nearby — pizza, sushi, fast food — without leaving home.

The people who deliver are called Dashers. They use their own cars and get paid per delivery. DoorDash is the biggest delivery app in America.

Founded in 2013 by Stanford students Tony Xu, Stanley Tang, Andy Fang, and Evan Moore after researching small business challenges in the Bay Area, DoorDash started with a focus on suburban markets — a different strategy from early competitor Seamless which focused on dense urban areas. This suburban bet proved transformative.

DoorDash went public on the NYSE in December 2020, raising $3.4 billion in one of the year's largest tech IPOs. The company commands approximately 67% of US food delivery market share, having surpassed Grubhub and Uber Eats through aggressive merchant recruitment, driver incentives, and consumer marketing.

Beyond restaurant delivery, DoorDash has expanded into grocery, convenience, alcohol, and even pet supplies through partnerships with retailers including Walgreens, 7-Eleven, and Petco. This vertical expansion positions DoorDash as a broader on-demand delivery platform rather than a food-specific service.

⚙️The DoorDash Business Model

DoorDash operates a three-sided marketplace connecting restaurants, Dashers, and consumers.

DoorDash earns revenue from three sources: commissions from restaurants (15-30% of order value), delivery fees from consumers, and DashPass subscription revenue. The commission structure has been a source of industry tension — many restaurants view delivery commissions as unsustainably high, particularly during COVID when delivery became essential.

DashPass, DoorDash's subscription membership at $9.99/month, offers free delivery and reduced fees from eligible restaurants. DashPass members order significantly more frequently than non-subscribers, making the subscription both a retention tool and a frequency driver.

DoorDash Ads (formerly Sponsored Listings) allows restaurants to pay for featured placement in DoorDash search results and category pages — a growing advertising revenue stream similar to Instacart Ads and Amazon Advertising. High-intent grocery and restaurant advertising is increasingly recognized as premium digital inventory.

💰Expansion and International Growth

DoorDash is extending its market leadership beyond the US.

DoorDash acquired Wolt, a European food delivery company, for $8.1 billion in 2022 — significantly expanding its international footprint into 23 European and international markets. Wolt operates as a separate brand but shares technology and operational learnings with DoorDash.

DoorDash Drive, the company's white-label delivery logistics service, allows retailers and restaurants to offer DoorDash-powered delivery under their own brand. This B2B product extends DoorDash's logistics capabilities to partners who want the infrastructure without the branded DoorDash customer relationship.

DoorDash Kitchen (ghost kitchens), where restaurants can operate delivery-only kitchen locations, represents another expansion of the business model — helping restaurant partners scale delivery capacity without traditional real estate costs.

📅History & Timeline

DoorDash's decade of food delivery leadership:

2013 — Founded at Stanford

Tony Xu and cofounders launch DoorDash from a Palo Alto macaroon store, focusing initially on suburban restaurant delivery.

2018 — Market Leader

DoorDash surpasses Grubhub to become the largest US food delivery platform by order volume.

2020 — NYSE IPO

DoorDash raises $3.4 billion in its NYSE IPO at a $39 billion valuation — one of 2020's largest tech public offerings.

2022 — Wolt Acquisition

DoorDash acquires European delivery platform Wolt for $8.1 billion, significantly expanding international presence to 25+ countries.

2023 — DashPass Milestone

DashPass surpasses 15 million subscribers, becoming one of the largest food and delivery subscription services globally.
