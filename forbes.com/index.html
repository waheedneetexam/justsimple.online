💼What is Forbes?

Forbes is one of the world's most recognizable business media brands, famous for its annual wealth rankings and authoritative coverage of business, finance, and entrepreneurship.

Explain it like I'm 10

Forbes is like a magazine that writes about rich people and successful businesses. Every year they make a list of the richest people in the world — the Forbes Billionaires List!

They also write articles about how businesses work, new inventions, and tips for people who want to start their own companies.

Founded in 1917 by B.C. Forbes, the publication built its reputation on business journalism and its iconic annual lists. The Forbes Billionaires List, launched in 1987, became a global cultural phenomenon and established Forbes as the arbiter of wealth and business success.

Today Forbes operates as a digital-first media company with forbes.com serving over 150 million monthly visitors. The site publishes thousands of articles monthly across business, technology, finance, lifestyle, and entrepreneurship, with contributions from a large network of paid contributors alongside staff journalists.

Forbes Media was sold to a group of investors including Integrated Whale Media in 2014, and in 2022, Forbes went public via SPAC merger. The brand has expanded into events, licensing, and branded content while maintaining its core editorial identity.

⚙️The Forbes Lists Phenomenon

Forbes annual rankings have become among the most influential and widely-cited lists in business culture.

The Forbes 400 (richest Americans) and Forbes Billionaires List are compiled by a dedicated research team that investigates and estimates net worth using public filings, asset valuations, and direct interviews. The methodology is rigorous enough to be taken seriously by financial markets.

Beyond wealth lists, Forbes publishes dozens of specialized rankings including the World's Most Powerful People, Best Employers, Most Innovative Companies, and sector-specific lists like the Midas List for top venture capitalists. These rankings drive significant traffic and media coverage.

Being featured on a Forbes list has become a badge of status in the business world. Companies actively pitch Forbes research teams for inclusion, and the lists are cited extensively in fundraising materials, press releases, and corporate communications worldwide.

💰Digital Transformation and Revenue

Forbes has successfully navigated the shift from print to digital media.

Forbes.com generates revenue through programmatic advertising, branded content (BrandVoice), events, and its Forbes membership program. BrandVoice allows companies to publish content under their brand within the Forbes ecosystem — a model that has been both lucrative and controversial among editorial purists.

Forbes Councils is a paid membership community where executives pay to publish thought leadership articles on Forbes.com. With thousands of members paying annual fees, this represents a significant and growing revenue stream that supplements traditional advertising.

The Forbes brand has been extended into licensing deals, Forbes-branded events and conferences, and international editions operated by local partners in markets including Asia, Europe, Latin America, and the Middle East.

📅History & Timeline

Forbes through the decades:

1917 — Founded

B.C. Forbes founds Forbes magazine in New York City with the mission of covering American business and capitalism.

1987 — Billionaires List

The first Forbes Billionaires List is published, introducing a new form of wealth journalism that becomes globally influential.

1996 — Forbes.com Launch

Forbes launches its website as one of the first major print business publications to establish a significant digital presence.

2014 — Ownership Change

The Forbes family sells a majority stake to Integrated Whale Media Investments, ending nearly a century of family ownership.

2022 — Public Company

Forbes goes public via a SPAC merger, becoming a publicly traded company valued at approximately $630 million.
