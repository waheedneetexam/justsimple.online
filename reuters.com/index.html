🌐What is Reuters?

Reuters is the world's largest and most trusted news agency, providing unbiased news content to newspapers, broadcasters, and financial institutions worldwide.

Explain it like I'm 10

Reuters is like the news factory that other news shows get their stories from. When something big happens anywhere in the world, Reuters reporters are usually there first to write about it.

Most newspapers and TV channels don't have reporters everywhere, so they pay Reuters to get their stories and share them with their readers.

Founded in 1851 by Paul Julius Reuter in London, Reuters pioneered the use of new communication technologies to deliver financial news faster than competitors. The agency's reputation for accuracy and impartiality has been its cornerstone for over 170 years.

Thomson Reuters, formed by the 2008 merger of the Thomson Corporation and Reuters Group, is now a massive information services company. Reuters News remains a distinct brand within Thomson Reuters, operating with editorial independence from the corporation's financial data businesses.

Reuters content reaches over one billion people daily through thousands of subscriber media organizations. The agency employs approximately 2,500 journalists and photographers in nearly 200 countries, making it one of the most geographically comprehensive news operations in existence.

⚙️News Wire Operations

Reuters operates one of the world's most sophisticated news distribution systems.

The Reuters wire service delivers thousands of news stories, photos, videos, and graphics every day to subscriber organizations. Content is produced in multiple languages and formatted for direct publication by client media organizations ranging from the New York Times to small regional newspapers.

Reuters Pictures is one of the world's premier photo agencies, with staff photographers and a global contributor network producing iconic images of major world events. Reuters video service similarly provides broadcast-quality footage to television networks worldwide.

Reuters financial data services, operating under the Thomson Reuters brand, provide real-time market data and analytics that compete directly with Bloomberg. This financial data business generates the majority of Thomson Reuters revenue and cross-subsidizes the news operation.

💰Editorial Standards and Trust

Reuters' reputation for impartiality is its most valuable asset.

The Reuters Trust Principles, established in 1941, require the agency to maintain independence and freedom from bias. These principles are legally binding on Thomson Reuters through a trust structure that prevents any single party from controlling the organization in a way that could compromise editorial independence.

Reuters has developed the Reuters Institute for the Study of Journalism at Oxford University and the Reuters Fact Check service as extensions of its commitment to journalism standards. These initiatives reinforce the brand's positioning as a trusted information source in an era of widespread misinformation.

The agency's business model — selling news to other media organizations rather than competing for advertising — incentivizes accuracy and neutrality. A wire service that gets facts wrong or shows obvious political bias risks losing subscriber revenue, creating structural alignment between commercial interests and journalistic integrity.

📅History & Timeline

Reuters through the ages:

1851 — Founded

Paul Julius Reuter establishes his news agency in London, using carrier pigeons and telegraph to deliver financial news faster than competitors.

1941 — Trust Principles

Reuters Trust Principles established to protect editorial independence, creating a unique governance structure unlike any other news organization.

1984 — Stock Market Listing

Reuters Group lists on the London and NASDAQ stock exchanges, raising capital for global expansion.

2008 — Thomson Reuters Merger

Reuters merges with The Thomson Corporation to form Thomson Reuters, one of the world's leading information services companies.

2018 — Blackstone Investment

Blackstone acquires a 55% stake in the Financial and Risk business (later renamed Refinitiv), reshaping Thomson Reuters' focus.
