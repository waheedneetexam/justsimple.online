Expedia: How One of the World's Largest Travel Agencies Works

An independent deep-dive into Expedia's online travel agency model, the merchant and agency booking economics, its portfolio of brands (Hotels.com, Vrbo, Orbitz), and competition with Booking.com in the $750B global travel market.

🌐 expedia.com

Editorial Independence: JustSimple.Online is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Expedia. All analysis is independent research for informational purposes only.

✈️What is Expedia?

Expedia is one of the world's largest online travel agencies — a platform where travellers book flights, hotels, car rentals, vacation packages, and experiences, while hotels and airlines pay commissions for reaching Expedia's customers.

Explain it like I'm 10

Expedia is a website where you can book flights, hotels, rental cars, and holiday packages all in one place. Instead of going to each airline and hotel website separately to compare prices, you can search on Expedia and it shows you options from hundreds of airlines and thousands of hotels. Expedia earns money by taking a commission from hotels and airlines each time someone books through Expedia. Hotels pay Expedia because Expedia brings them customers they might not have found on their own hotel website.

Expedia owns several other travel brands you might know: Hotels.com (hotels specifically), Vrbo (vacation home rentals, competing with Airbnb), Orbitz, Cheaptickets, Hotwire (for last-minute deals), and Wotif (Australia). By owning multiple travel booking websites, Expedia can reach different types of travellers — budget travellers through Hotwire, business travellers through Expedia, and vacation rental travellers through Vrbo.

Expedia was launched in 1996 as a division of Microsoft before spinning off as an independent company in 1999. Microsoft's early internet investment in travel booking was prescient — online travel became one of the largest e-commerce categories. Expedia pioneered the online travel agency model where travellers could search, compare, and book travel without calling a travel agent. The platform grew rapidly as internet penetration increased and consumers embraced online booking for its transparency and convenience.

Expedia Group has assembled a portfolio of travel brands through acquisitions: Hotels.com (2001), Classic Vacations (2002), Hotwire (2003), Orbitz (2015), HomeAway/Vrbo (2015, $3.9B), Cheaptickets, and international properties. This multi-brand portfolio allows Expedia to capture different traveller segments and price points under separate brand identities while sharing common back-end infrastructure, hotel/airline content, and booking technology.

Vrbo (Vacation Rentals By Owner) is Expedia's strategic investment in the vacation rental market created by Airbnb. Vrbo focuses on whole-property rentals (entire homes, not rooms) in family vacation destinations — a positioning distinct from Airbnb's broader accommodation model. Vrbo represents Expedia's largest growth investment and is critical to competing in the vacation rental category that took significant market share from traditional hotel bookings.

⚙️How Expedia Works

Expedia's booking model connects travellers with hotels, airlines, and other suppliers through two distinct revenue arrangements: agency model (commissions) and merchant model (markup).

Expedia operates two booking models. In the agency model, Expedia acts as the booking agent for the hotel or airline — the traveller pays the supplier directly at check-in, and Expedia receives a commission (typically 10-20% for hotels). In the merchant model (more common), Expedia prepays hotels for rooms at a contracted rate and resells them to travellers at a markup, collecting the full payment upfront. The merchant model provides Expedia with higher revenue control but also inventory risk (unsold hotel rooms). The choice of model varies by supplier relationship and market conditions.

Expedia's package booking feature (combining flights and hotels) is one of its most profitable products. Packages create bundling value for travellers (lower combined price than booking separately) while providing Expedia with higher total booking revenue per customer. The opaque pricing in some bundle products (travellers see total price but not individual component prices) protects supplier pricing integrity while allowing discounting. Packages also reduce the influence of price-comparison meta-search by making it harder to compare package components individually.

One Key is Expedia's loyalty programme unifying rewards across Expedia.com, Hotels.com, and Vrbo — allowing members to earn and redeem OneKeyCash across all brands. The unified loyalty programme incentivises multi-brand usage within Expedia's portfolio (earn on Hotels.com, redeem on Vrbo) and reduces churn to Booking.com by creating cross-brand switching costs within the Expedia ecosystem.

💰How Expedia Makes Money

Expedia generates revenue through hotel and vacation rental commissions/markups, flight booking fees, and advertising from travel suppliers.

Hotel booking revenue (commissions and markups) is Expedia's largest revenue segment. Hotels pay 15-25% commissions to Expedia for booking referrals, or accept merchant model pre-purchased room blocks at contracted rates. With millions of hotel nights booked annually, commission revenue represents the bulk of Expedia's $12B+ revenue. Large hotel chains negotiate lower commission rates due to volume; independent hotels often pay higher rates for access to Expedia's customer base.

Vrbo vacation rental commissions (approximately 5-15% of booking value from property owners and/or guests) represent a growing segment as vacation rental bookings grow relative to hotel bookings. Vrbo's whole-home rental positioning gives it higher average booking values than hotel rooms, making commission revenue per booking higher.

Flight booking fees (from airlines and service fees charged to travellers), car rental commissions, and Expedia Media Solutions (advertising sold to travel suppliers promoting their properties within Expedia's search results) contribute additional revenue. Expedia's loyalty programme drives repeat bookings that reduce customer acquisition costs per booking — loyal members who book through Expedia repeatedly have significantly lower CAC than one-time buyers acquired through search advertising.

✨What Makes Expedia Unique

Expedia's multi-brand travel portfolio, One Key loyalty currency, and global scale create a travel booking ecosystem that serves travellers across all segments and trip types.

🏨

Multi-Brand Portfolio

Expedia.com, Hotels.com, Vrbo, Orbitz, Hotwire, Cheaptickets under one group. Each brand serves different traveller segments while sharing booking infrastructure.

🏠

Vrbo Whole-Home Rentals

Strategic response to Airbnb. Vrbo focuses on whole-home vacation rentals, particularly for families — a distinct market segment with higher average booking values than shared accommodation.

🎁

One Key Loyalty

Unified loyalty currency across Expedia, Hotels.com, and Vrbo. Cross-brand earning and redemption creates switching costs within the Expedia ecosystem.

📦

Package Bookings

Flight + hotel bundling creates value for travellers and reduces exposure to price-comparison meta-search. Packages represent one of Expedia's highest-margin booking types.

🌍

200+ Country Global Scale

Expedia operates in 200+ countries with localised pricing, currency, and content. Global scale provides access to inventory breadth that regional competitors cannot match.

💡

Trivago and Meta-Search Investment

Expedia invested in Trivago (hotel price comparison meta-search) to participate in the meta-search traffic layer between Google and OTA booking, capturing demand at multiple funnel stages.

📅History & Timeline

From a 1996 Microsoft division to one of the world's largest travel companies.

1996 — Expedia Launches as Microsoft Division

Expedia.com launches as an online travel booking division within Microsoft.

1999 — Expedia Spins Off and IPOs

Expedia spins off from Microsoft and lists publicly. Online travel booking market growing rapidly as internet adoption increases.

2001 — Hotels.com Acquisition

Expedia acquires Hotels.com, beginning the multi-brand portfolio strategy.

2015 — HomeAway/Vrbo Acquisition

Expedia acquires HomeAway (including Vrbo) for $3.9B, entering the vacation rental market in direct competition with Airbnb.

2020 — COVID-19 Travel Collapse

Expedia loses $2.9B in 2020 as global travel halts. Restructures with significant cost reductions.

2023 — One Key Loyalty Launch

Expedia launches One Key unified loyalty programme across Expedia, Hotels.com, and Vrbo, creating cross-brand switching costs.
