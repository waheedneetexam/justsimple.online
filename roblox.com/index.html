Roblox: How the User-Created Gaming Metaverse Works

An independent deep-dive into Roblox's Lua scripting engine, Robux virtual economy, creator monetisation, and how it became the most played gaming platform for users under 17.

🌐 roblox.com

Editorial Independence: JustSimple.Online is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Roblox. All analysis is independent research for informational purposes only.

🧱What is Roblox?

Roblox is not one game — it's a platform where players can play millions of games all made by other players, using a virtual currency that converts to real money.

Explain it like I'm 10

Roblox is like a giant gaming shopping mall where every game inside was made by another player, not a professional game company. There are millions of different games on Roblox — obstacle courses, simulators, role-playing games, horror games, and even games that recreate real places. Some games have millions of players; others are made by individual kids who learned to code through Roblox's tools.

Roblox has its own money called Robux. You buy Robux with real money, then spend them on accessories for your character, passes to unlock content in games, or special abilities. Game creators can earn Robux from players in their games, and eventually convert those Robux back into real money. Some teenagers have earned hundreds of thousands of dollars by making popular Roblox games.

Roblox Corporation was founded in 2004 by David Baszucki and Erik Cassel as DynaBlocks, renaming to Roblox in 2005. The platform was built on the premise that users should be able to create and share their own games — a concept years ahead of the mainstream user-generated content movement. Roblox uses a simplified version of Lua as its scripting language, making game creation accessible to young developers.

Roblox's growth trajectory changed dramatically during the COVID-19 pandemic. With schools closed and children at home, daily active users grew from 17 million in 2019 to 37 million in 2020. The platform went public in March 2021 via direct listing at a $45 billion valuation. Roblox has responded to post-pandemic normalisation by investing in expanding its user base beyond under-13 toward teenagers and young adults with more sophisticated content.

Roblox's creator economy is unique in gaming. The Roblox Developer Exchange (DevEx) programme allows creators who earn Robux to exchange them for US dollars. Top Roblox game developers earn millions of dollars annually from their game creations. Over 2.5 million active creators contribute games to the platform, of which approximately 3,200 earned over $10,000 in 2023.

⚙️How Roblox Works

Roblox's platform combines a physics engine, Lua scripting environment, social infrastructure, and a virtual economy into a single creation and play platform.

Roblox Studio is the game creation tool available free to all users. It provides a drag-and-drop 3D environment builder, a Lua IDE for scripting game logic, a physics engine for realistic object behaviour, and built-in testing. Roblox's Lua scripting API exposes objects (Parts, Humanoids, Models), events (touched, clicked, joined), and services (DataStore for persistence, MessagingService for inter-server communication). While simplified compared to Unity or Unreal, it's a genuine programming environment.

Roblox's server infrastructure must handle 88 million daily active users simultaneously across millions of individual game instances. Each Roblox experience runs in its own server instance, isolated from other games. Roblox's cloud infrastructure dynamically provisions game servers based on demand — a popular game might run thousands of simultaneous instances. The DataStore service provides persistent storage for player data (inventory, progress) across sessions.

Roblox's avatar system and UGC Marketplace are key engagement and monetisation drivers. Players customise their avatars with hats, faces, clothing, and accessories — both official Roblox items and user-created items sold through the UGC Marketplace. Creators can design and sell accessories as 3D models, earning Robux on every purchase. The avatar customisation economy generates significant Robux flow and creates a fashion layer on top of gaming.

💰How Roblox Makes Money

Roblox earns revenue from every Robux purchase, taking a margin between the player's buy-in price and the creator's payout rate.

Robux sales are Roblox's primary revenue source. Players buy Robux at approximately $0.01-0.0125 per Robux, spend them on game passes, avatar items, and Premium access. Creators are paid out through DevEx at $0.0035 per Robux — meaning Roblox earns the spread between the $0.01 buy-in and the $0.0035 payout. In 2023, Roblox's revenue was $2.7B in Robux sales, against $741M paid out to creators.

Roblox Premium subscription ($4.99, $9.99, or $19.99/month) provides a monthly Robux stipend and exclusive in-game perks in participating experiences. The subscription benefits compound: Premium members have ongoing Robux income to spend, making them more engaged players who generate more revenue for creators.

Roblox's Advertising products (Roblox Ads, sponsored experiences, immersive ads within games) are a growing revenue stream, particularly for brands wanting to reach the under-25 demographic. Brands including Nike, Gucci, and Vans have launched branded Roblox experiences, paying Roblox for development and promotional placement.

📅History & Timeline

From DynaBlocks to the world's largest user-created gaming platform.

2004 — Founded as DynaBlocks

David Baszucki and Erik Cassel launch as DynaBlocks, a physics-simulation education tool that becomes the foundation for Roblox.

2005 — Roblox Launches

Renames to Roblox and launches publicly. First user-created games appear on the platform.

2013 — Robux Economy Matures

DevEx program launches, allowing creators to convert Robux to cash for the first time. The creator economy is born.

2020 — COVID Boom — 37M Daily Users

Daily active users more than double during COVID. Roblox becomes a primary social and entertainment platform for children globally.

2021 — IPO at $45B Valuation

Direct listing on NYSE. Roblox's metaverse narrative makes it one of the most talked-about tech listings of the year.

2024 — 88M Daily Users and Adult Expansion

Continues growing beyond the core under-13 demographic. Immersive advertising becomes a significant new revenue category.
