Cricbuzz: How the World's Most Popular Cricket Site Works

🏏What is Cricbuzz?

Cricbuzz is cricket's digital home — the site where hundreds of millions of fans follow every ball, every wicket, and every match in real time.

Explain it like I'm 10

Cricket is one of the most popular sports in the world, especially in India, Pakistan, Australia, England, and the Caribbean. Cricbuzz is the website and app that cricket fans use to follow live match scores when they can't watch the game on TV. You can see the score update ball by ball — almost instantly after each delivery is bowled — so you always know what's happening even if you're in school or at work.

Cricbuzz also has articles about cricket news, player statistics, match previews, and expert opinions. When big matches like IPL or World Cup games are on, tens of millions of people are checking Cricbuzz at the same time. It's like a cricket Twitter, scoreboard, and newspaper all in one.

Cricbuzz was founded in 2004 by Pankaj Chhaparwal and Pravin Vidhani, and was acquired by Times Internet (the digital arm of The Times of India Group) in 2014. Times Internet, part of Bennett, Coleman and Co. Ltd. — India's largest media conglomerate — has provided Cricbuzz with the distribution infrastructure and financial backing to scale into the world's most visited cricket destination.

Cricbuzz's core product is ball-by-ball live scoring — a real-time data feed that updates the scorecard within seconds of each delivery being bowled. For a cricket match, this means hundreds of data points per hour: runs scored, wickets fallen, extras, required run rate, player statistics, wagon wheel, and pitch maps. This level of granularity for every ball of every match — from international Tests to domestic T20 leagues across 50+ countries — requires a massive data collection operation.

Cricbuzz operates at extraordinary scale during major tournaments. During the IPL or ICC Cricket World Cup, Cricbuzz handles hundreds of millions of page views per day. During a tense India-Pakistan match, real-time traffic can exceed what most websites experience in a month. The platform supports 7 languages (English, Hindi, Bangla, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam) reflecting cricket's linguistic diversity in South Asia.

⚙️How Cricbuzz Works

Cricbuzz's real-time scoring requires trained scorers at grounds, data processing infrastructure, and a globally distributed delivery system.

Cricbuzz employs trained live scorers at cricket venues who input ball-by-ball data into Cricbuzz's scoring software in near-real-time. Each scorer records the ball's outcome (runs, wicket, wide, no-ball) along with additional information (shot type, fielding position). This data enters Cricbuzz's processing pipeline, which computes all dependent statistics and pushes the updated scorecard to all connected clients within 1-3 seconds. For major international matches, Cricbuzz receives official data feeds from ICC/BCCI alongside its own scorers as a redundancy layer.

The Cricbuzz mobile app uses persistent WebSocket connections to push score updates to users without requiring page refreshes. This push architecture is critical for the user experience: cricket fans expect to glance at their phone and see the current score without tapping. During peak traffic (last overs of a close IPL match), the push notification and WebSocket systems must handle hundreds of millions of concurrent connections.

Cricbuzz's news and analysis content is produced by a dedicated editorial team covering cricket journalism — player interviews, match previews, team news, and opinion columns. This editorial content drives SEO traffic beyond live scores, attracting users during non-match periods through cricket news searches. The combination of real-time scores and editorial content makes Cricbuzz a round-the-clock cricket destination.

💰How Cricbuzz Makes Money

Cricbuzz monetises its massive cricket audience through advertising and its premium subscription tier.

Digital advertising is Cricbuzz's primary revenue source. With 100M+ monthly visitors and high engagement (cricket fans check scores repeatedly throughout a match day), Cricbuzz's ad inventory is among the most valuable in South Asian digital media. Display ads, video pre-rolls, and sponsored content from cricket equipment brands, payment apps, and telecom providers generate the bulk of revenue. CPMs are elevated during IPL season.

Cricbuzz Premium subscription offers ad-free browsing, detailed player analytics, expert predictions, and exclusive video content for paying subscribers. The subscription is priced to attract the most engaged cricket fans who check scores frequently enough that the ad experience becomes irritating. Subscription revenue provides a predictable recurring revenue stream.

As part of Times Internet, Cricbuzz benefits from shared advertising infrastructure, audience data, and cross-promotion across Times Internet's portfolio (Times of India, Economic Times, Gaana, MX Player). Advertisers who buy campaigns across Times Internet properties often include Cricbuzz in the package deal. Cricbuzz also licenses its live scoring data and APIs to other apps and sports data aggregators.
