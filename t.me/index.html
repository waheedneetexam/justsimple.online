Telegram: How the Privacy-First Messaging Giant Works

An independent deep-dive into Telegram's MTProto protocol, channel architecture, TON blockchain, business model, and Pavel Durov's vision for decentralised communication.

Editorial Independence: JustSimple.Online is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Telegram. All analysis is independent research for informational purposes only.

✈️What is Telegram?

Telegram is the messaging app that became a platform — combining private chats, mass broadcast channels, 200,000-member groups, bots, and its own blockchain into one app used by nearly 1 billion people.

Explain it like I'm 10

Telegram is a messaging app like WhatsApp or iMessage, but with superpowers. You can send messages to one person or to a million people at once through something called a Channel. You can join groups with up to 200,000 members. And you can use small programs called Bots inside Telegram — for example, a bot that tells you the weather, or one that lets you buy things with cryptocurrency.

Telegram was made by Pavel Durov, the same person who created VK (Russia's Facebook). He left Russia because the government wanted him to spy on users, and he made Telegram specifically so that no government could easily read people's private messages. Many people around the world use it because they trust it more than apps owned by Facebook or Chinese companies.

Telegram was founded in 2013 by Pavel Durov and his brother Nikolai Durov, after Pavel's forced departure from VK (VKontakte). The brothers developed the MTProto encryption protocol specifically for Telegram, prioritising speed and security over the Signal Protocol used by WhatsApp. Telegram was initially self-funded by Pavel Durov from his VK sale proceeds, and for years operated with no revenue while growing to hundreds of millions of users. The platform is headquartered in Dubai, UAE, having relocated from Berlin.

Telegram's differentiation from WhatsApp and iMessage lies in its public infrastructure. While WhatsApp is primarily a private messaging tool, Telegram functions as a hybrid between a messaging app and a publishing platform. Telegram Channels allow a single account to broadcast to an unlimited number of subscribers — effectively functioning as RSS feeds, news channels, or entertainment feeds within the app. Channels with millions of subscribers are common, making Telegram a major content distribution platform in addition to a messaging service.

Telegram's growth has been especially pronounced in markets with internet restrictions or political instability. During protests in Belarus, Iran, Ukraine, and Hong Kong, Telegram served as a primary communication tool for coordinating activities outside government surveillance. This political utility, combined with genuinely useful technical features (large file transfers up to 4GB, cross-platform sync, bots, and channels), has driven organic growth to 950M+ monthly active users. In July 2024, Pavel Durov was arrested in France on charges related to Telegram's moderation practices, raising new questions about the platform's governance.

⚙️How Telegram Works

Telegram's MTProto protocol and cloud-sync architecture deliver speed and security at a scale few messaging apps can match.

Telegram uses its proprietary MTProto protocol for all client-server communication. MTProto was designed by Nikolai Durov and combines RSA-2048, Diffie-Hellman, and AES-256 encryption in a way optimised for low-latency delivery on unreliable mobile networks. Unlike Signal Protocol (used by WhatsApp and Signal), MTProto stores messages in Telegram's cloud, enabling multi-device sync. This is a deliberate trade-off: Telegram's cloud messages are server-side encrypted (Telegram can decrypt them) while Secret Chats use end-to-end encryption with no server-side copy.

Telegram's bot API is one of its most powerful technical features. Any developer can create a Telegram bot — a special account controlled by software — that can respond to messages, run commands, accept payments, send notifications, and interact with external services. Millions of bots exist for purposes ranging from spam filtering to cryptocurrency trading to customer service. The Bot API is a documented REST API that has driven massive third-party development, effectively making Telegram a programmable platform.

The TON (The Open Network) blockchain is Telegram's most ambitious technical project. Originally cancelled after an SEC enforcement action against Telegram's $1.7B ICO, TON was revived as a community project and re-integrated into Telegram as the native payment and identity layer. TON allows Telegram users to send Toncoin (TON) peer-to-peer, purchase Telegram Premium, buy @username handles, and access decentralised apps (DApps) directly within Telegram. This blockchain integration transforms Telegram from a communication app into a financial infrastructure platform.

💰How Telegram Makes Money

After years of operating at a loss, Telegram launched multiple revenue streams in 2022 — while maintaining its core service free for all users.

Telegram Premium ($4.99/month) launched in 2022 and gives subscribers larger file uploads (4GB vs 2GB), faster downloads, exclusive stickers and reactions, no advertising, and the ability to follow more channels. Telegram reported $700M in revenue from Premium subscriptions in 2024 with 10+ million paying subscribers. The Premium model follows the Telegram philosophy of keeping core features free while charging power users for enhanced capabilities.

Username and channel handle auctions via Fragment (a TON blockchain marketplace) allow users to bid on premium @usernames using Toncoin. High-value usernames like @bitcoin or @crypto have sold for hundreds of thousands of dollars. Telegram takes a commission from each auction, and the system has generated tens of millions of dollars in revenue while also serving as a launch platform for TON blockchain adoption.

Telegram launched ads in public channels in 2021 — a carefully limited advertising product where channel owners with 1000+ subscribers can receive ad revenue sharing when Telegram shows sponsored messages in their channels. The ad product is opt-in for channel owners and shown only in large public channels, not in private chats or groups. This preserves user trust while generating revenue from the public broadcasting use case. TON-based payments and DApp commissions represent a growing revenue category as the blockchain ecosystem matures.

✨What Makes Telegram Unique

Telegram combines messaging, broadcasting, bots, and blockchain in ways no competitor has replicated.

📢

Channels

Broadcast to unlimited subscribers. Major news outlets, crypto projects, and governments use Telegram Channels as a primary publishing platform.

🤖

Bot API

Open API lets anyone build bots inside Telegram. Millions of bots exist for customer service, crypto trading, games, and notifications — making Telegram a programmable platform.

🔒

Secret Chats

True end-to-end encryption with self-destruct timers and no server-side copy. The highest privacy option for sensitive communications.

💎

TON Blockchain

Built-in cryptocurrency wallet, username auctions, and DApp access. Telegram is the only major messaging app with a native blockchain layer.

📂

Large File Transfers

Send files up to 4GB (Premium) — larger than any competing messaging platform. Makes Telegram a practical file-sharing tool for professionals.

🌐

No Phone Number Required

Telegram recently introduced username-based login — users can now join without sharing a phone number, increasing privacy significantly.

📅History & Timeline

From a Russian exile's privacy project to a near-billion-user global platform.

2013 — Launch After VK Departure

Pavel Durov launches Telegram from Berlin after leaving Russia. Early focus on speed and security compared to WhatsApp.

2017 — Channels Transform into Publishers

Telegram Channels become major news distribution platforms, especially in Russia, Iran, and Eastern Europe.

2018 — $1.7B ICO Cancelled by SEC

Telegram raises $1.7B for the TON blockchain but the SEC halts the project. Telegram refunds investors and abandons the original TON.

2020 — 700M Users During COVID

Lockdowns drive massive messaging app growth. Telegram benefits from WhatsApp backlash over Facebook ownership.

2022 — Telegram Premium Launches

First revenue-generating product. Proves the freemium model works at Telegram's scale.

2024 — Durov Arrested in France

Pavel Durov detained in Paris on charges related to content moderation. Telegram updates policies; debate over platform responsibility intensifies.
