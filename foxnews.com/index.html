Fox News: How America's Most-Watched Cable News Channel Works

An independent deep-dive into Fox News's cable carriage fee and advertising revenue model, its dominant ratings in US cable news, the Fox News Digital strategy, and how the network built an unrivalled conservative news audience.

📺What is Fox News?

Fox News Channel is America's most-watched cable news network — a 24-hour news and commentary channel that dominates US cable news ratings with a conservative-leaning editorial perspective.

Explain it like I'm 10

Fox News is a TV channel that shows news 24 hours a day in America. It's the most watched news channel in the US, especially by older Americans and conservatives. Fox News was created in 1996 by Rupert Murdoch's company and is very popular because it gives a conservative point of view on American politics and news — most other major news channels are seen as more liberal or moderate. Shows like Fox and Friends, Hannity, and Tucker Carlson Tonight have made some Fox hosts very famous.

Fox News makes money in two ways: advertising (companies paying to show ads to Fox viewers) and cable carriage fees (every household that has cable TV pays a small fee each month that goes partly to Fox News, even if they never watch it). Because Fox News is in over 90 million cable homes and has the highest ratings of any cable news channel, it earns billions of dollars each year from these two sources combined.

Fox News Channel launched on October 7, 1996 under the leadership of Roger Ailes, recruited by Rupert Murdoch's News Corporation to create an alternative to CNN that would serve conservative American audiences. Ailes correctly identified that a large American audience felt underserved by mainstream news coverage they perceived as having a liberal bias, and designed Fox News's programming (fast-paced, patriotic, personality-driven) to attract and retain that audience. Fox News overtook CNN in cable news ratings by 2002 and has maintained dominance since.

Fox News's ratings dominance is built on a primetime opinion and commentary lineup — shows like Hannity, The Ingraham Angle, and The Story with Martha MacCallum — that blur the boundary between news reporting and conservative political commentary. This opinion format drives higher emotional engagement and loyalty than traditional objective news reporting, creating a loyal viewer base that watches daily and returns repeatedly. Fox News's primetime shows routinely beat all other cable news programmes in total viewers.

FoxNews.com and the Fox News app extend the network's reach digitally. The site receives 80M+ monthly unique visitors and offers live streaming, breaking news, video clips, and original digital content. Fox News Digital has grown significantly as younger conservatives who don't watch linear TV access Fox News content through digital channels. The Fox News app has been consistently one of the most downloaded news apps in the US.

⚙️How Fox News Works

Fox News operates a 24-hour news and opinion cable channel built on daytime news programming and high-rated primetime opinion shows that drive the network's advertising and carriage fee revenue.

Fox News's programming schedule divides into daytime news (objective breaking news coverage with traditional reporting) and primetime opinion (personality-driven commentary programmes with a conservative editorial perspective). Daytime anchors like Bret Baier and Martha MacCallum provide traditional news coverage. Primetime hosts like Sean Hannity, Laura Ingraham, and Jesse Watters present commentary mixed with news. This structure allows Fox to claim both news credibility (through its reporting hours) and drive ratings (through its opinion hours).

Fox News's talent star system is central to its programming strategy. Hosts like Tucker Carlson (until 2023), Sean Hannity, and Megyn Kelly (until 2017) develop loyal personal audiences who follow their shows daily. When a Fox News host departs, their replacement must rebuild that audience loyalty — host transitions are strategically significant. Fox News has demonstrated the ability to retain audience share through host transitions by maintaining consistent editorial positioning and format.

Fox News Digital (foxnews.com, Fox News App, Fox Nation subscription streaming) extends the brand beyond linear TV. Fox Nation is Fox News's subscription streaming service offering exclusive shows, documentaries, and content from Fox News personalities not available on the linear channel. It represents Fox's investment in direct-to-consumer streaming as part of a cable transition strategy similar to ESPN's ESPN+ approach.

💰How Fox News Makes Money

Fox News generates revenue from cable carriage fees (distribution) and advertising — the same dual-revenue model as ESPN and other cable networks.

Cable carriage fees are paid by every US cable and satellite household as part of their package, regardless of whether they watch Fox News. Fox News charges approximately $2.00+ per subscriber per month in carriage fees — significantly less than ESPN's $9 but reaching 90M+ households. This generates approximately $2B+ annually in carriage fee revenue from distributors like Comcast, DirecTV, and Charter. Carriage fee negotiations are conducted periodically and occasionally result in public disputes when fee increases are contested.

Advertising revenue is driven by Fox News's cable news ratings dominance. The network's primetime shows (Hannity, The Ingraham Angle, Gutfeld!) consistently beat CNN and MSNBC in total viewers by margins of 2-3x. Advertisers pay higher CPMs for Fox's larger and more engaged audience. Fox News's audience demographics (older, higher homeownership rates) are valuable for financial services, pharmaceutical, and insurance advertisers who dominate cable news advertising.

Fox Nation subscription revenue (approximately $5.99-$8.99/month) provides a direct-to-consumer revenue stream supplementing cable revenue. Digital advertising on FoxNews.com and the app adds additional revenue. Fox News is part of Fox Corporation (split from 21st Century Fox in 2019 when Disney acquired Fox's entertainment assets), which also operates Fox Sports, Fox Business Network, and owned-and-operated television stations.

📅History & Timeline

From a 1996 launch to 20+ years of cable news dominance.

1996 — Fox News Channel Launches

Rupert Murdoch and Roger Ailes launch Fox News Channel on October 7, 1996. Initial distribution is limited but grows rapidly through aggressive cable carriage negotiations.

2002 — Fox News Overtakes CNN

Fox News surpasses CNN in cable news ratings for the first time. Maintains ratings leadership continuously since.

2016 — Roger Ailes Departs Amid Scandal

Fox News founder Roger Ailes exits amid sexual harassment allegations. The network demonstrates it can maintain ratings leadership without its founding editorial director.

2017 — Bill O'Reilly Dismissal

Fox News's highest-rated host Bill O'Reilly is dismissed following sexual harassment allegations and advertiser boycott. Network maintains ratings through Tucker Carlson's rise.

2019 — Fox Corporation Split

Disney acquires Fox's entertainment assets; Fox News becomes part of the new Fox Corporation alongside Fox Sports and Fox broadcast network.

2023 — Dominion Defamation Settlement

Fox News settles Dominion Voting Systems defamation lawsuit for $787.5M over 2020 election coverage. Tucker Carlson departs the network in the same period.
