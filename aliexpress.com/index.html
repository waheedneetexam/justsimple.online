AliExpress: How Alibaba's Global Marketplace Works

An independent deep-dive into AliExpress's seller ecosystem, buyer protection programme, logistics network, and how it made Chinese manufacturing directly accessible to consumers worldwide.

🌐 aliexpress.com

🛒What is AliExpress?

AliExpress is like Amazon, but you're buying directly from Chinese and global manufacturers — often at dramatically lower prices because the middlemen are eliminated.

Explain it like I'm 10

AliExpress is a huge online shop where you can buy almost anything — phone cases, clothes, toys, electronics, tools — directly from the companies that make them, mostly in China. Because you're buying from the manufacturer rather than a store that bought from the manufacturer, the prices are often very low. The catch is that delivery can take 2-4 weeks since the items ship from China.

AliExpress is part of Alibaba, which is the biggest e-commerce company in China. It's different from Alibaba's main Chinese shopping site because it's designed for international buyers who don't speak Chinese. Over 150 million people around the world shop on AliExpress, particularly in Europe, Southeast Asia, and South America.

AliExpress was launched in 2010 by Alibaba Group as a global B2C (business-to-consumer) marketplace designed to give international buyers direct access to Chinese manufacturers and suppliers. Unlike Alibaba's B2B platform (alibaba.com) which connects business buyers with factories in bulk, AliExpress allows individual consumers to purchase single items at factory-adjacent prices. The platform launched at a time when Chinese manufacturing scale was becoming accessible to global consumers through the internet.

AliExpress operates as a third-party marketplace — Alibaba does not hold inventory but connects buyers with millions of sellers (primarily Chinese but increasingly global sellers). The platform's value proposition is price: because many sellers are manufacturers or distributors at the source of supply chains, products are priced significantly below what Western retailers charge for the same items. For commodity products — phone accessories, basic clothing, home goods — AliExpress price advantages can be 50-90% compared to Western retail.

AliExpress has grown to over 150 million active international buyers across 220+ countries, making it the world's most visited e-commerce site outside China. The platform has invested heavily in logistics infrastructure (Cainiao Network) and in localisation for key markets, including establishing local warehouses in Russia (a top market), Europe, and Southeast Asia to reduce delivery times from weeks to days for popular product categories.

⚙️How AliExpress Works

AliExpress connects international buyers with sellers through a platform that handles discovery, payment, and dispute resolution across 220+ countries.

AliExpress's seller ecosystem is open to both Chinese and international sellers who register and meet Alibaba's seller requirements. Sellers pay a deposit and annual store fee for storefront access, then list products with their own photos, descriptions, and pricing. AliExpress handles payment processing through Alipay's international payment system, holding buyer funds in escrow until the buyer confirms delivery or the protection period expires. The seller ecosystem has expanded from pure Chinese manufacturers to include local sellers in Europe and the US who use AliExpress to reach international buyers.

AliExpress's logistics system is built around Cainiao Network, Alibaba's logistics subsidiary. Standard AliExpress shipping uses China Post or Cainiao's own logistics routes, which are cost-optimised but slow (15-45 days to most destinations). AliExpress Standard Shipping and AliExpress Premium Shipping offer faster options (7-15 days) at higher cost. Local warehouses in Russia, Spain, and Poland allow sellers who pre-position inventory to offer 3-7 day delivery in those markets, making AliExpress more competitive with Amazon for European buyers.

AliExpress Buyer Protection is central to the platform's trust mechanism. Buyers are covered against non-delivery, items significantly different from description, and damaged goods — with refund processing through Alipay. The dispute resolution system handles millions of claims annually. Seller performance metrics (ratings, response time, dispute rates) determine search ranking and seller badge status (Excellent Seller, Top Brand), creating incentives for quality service that reduce Alibaba's customer service costs.

💰How AliExpress Makes Money

AliExpress earns revenue from seller commissions, advertising, and value-added services for its merchant ecosystem.

Seller commissions of 5-8% on every completed transaction are AliExpress's primary revenue source. With $50B+ in annual GMV, even a 5% commission rate generates $2.5B+ in marketplace fees. Category commission rates vary (5% for electronics to 8%+ for fashion). Sellers also pay annual store subscription fees ($1,500-$10,000 depending on store type) for access to the platform.

Paid advertising within AliExpress (sponsored product listings, banner placements, search advertising) is a growing revenue stream. Sellers pay for visibility in search results and category pages, similar to Amazon Sponsored Products. During major shopping events (AliExpress's Super Deals, AliExpress Birthday Sale, 11.11 Global Shopping Festival), advertising spend by sellers spikes dramatically.

AliExpress's logistics revenue through Cainiao Network adds a transportation and fulfilment revenue stream separate from the marketplace commission. Value-added services for sellers — analytics tools, photography studios, translation services, and localisation support — contribute additional B2B revenue. AliExpress Choice, a curated programme where Alibaba selects top-quality products and guarantees 10-day delivery, represents a strategic evolution toward a more controlled, Amazon-like model.

📅History & Timeline

From Alibaba's international extension to the world's most visited global marketplace.

2010 — AliExpress Launches

Alibaba launches AliExpress as a global B2C marketplace, giving international buyers direct access to Chinese manufacturers for the first time.

2013 — Russia Becomes Top Market

Russia emerges as AliExpress's largest non-Chinese market. Chinese electronics and clothing at Russian-market prices drive explosive growth.

2016 — AliExpress Mobile App Launch

Mobile-first redesign and app launch. Mobile becomes the dominant channel for AliExpress globally within two years.

2018 — Local Warehouses in Europe

Cainiao opens local warehouses in Spain and Russia. 5-7 day delivery becomes possible for pre-positioned inventory.

2022 — AliExpress Choice Launch

Launches curated AliExpress Choice programme with guaranteed 10-day delivery — moving toward a more controlled Amazon-like model.

2024 — Competition from Temu and Shein

New Chinese-origin marketplaces Temu and Shein challenge AliExpress in Western markets. AliExpress responds with faster delivery and curation.
