📌What is Pinterest?

Not quite a social network, not quite a search engine — Pinterest occupies a unique niche as the internet's visual discovery and inspiration platform.

Explain it like I'm 10

Imagine you have a giant corkboard on your wall. You go around the internet cutting out pictures of things you like — a bedroom design, a cake recipe, a cool outfit — and pinning them to your board so you can find them later. Pinterest is exactly that, but online and with billions of images. You can make as many boards as you want, organise them by topic, and also look at what boards other people have made to get new ideas.

The clever part is that Pinterest remembers what kinds of things you pin and uses that to show you more pictures you'd probably like. So the more you use it, the better it gets at finding things that match your taste. It's like having a personal mood board that also gives you suggestions.

Pinterest, Inc. is an American visual discovery platform founded in March 2010 by Ben Silbermann, Paul Sciarra, and Evan Sharp. The core idea emerged from Silbermann's childhood love of collecting — he wanted to create a digital equivalent of the physical collections people maintain, whether stamps, recipes, or design inspiration. The name combines "pin" (to fasten something to a board) and "interest," reflecting the concept of cataloguing things you care about visually.

Pinterest launched publicly in 2010 and grew explosively, driven primarily by women interested in home décor, fashion, food, and weddings. By 2012 it had become one of the fastest-growing websites in internet history and briefly surpassed Twitter in referral traffic to external websites. This traffic-driving capability made it uniquely valuable to retailers and publishers who found Pinterest users were highly likely to purchase items they discovered on the platform — a behaviour pattern distinct from any other social platform.

The company went public on the New York Stock Exchange in April 2019 at a $10 billion valuation. Since then it has focused on transforming its massive visual index into a performance advertising business. Unlike Instagram or TikTok, Pinterest is explicitly focused on future planning — people pin things they want to do, buy, make, or become — which makes its advertising exceptionally effective because users arrive with genuine purchase intent rather than passive entertainment consumption.

⚙️How Does Pinterest Work?

Pinterest's technology stack blends computer vision, graph-based recommendations, and visual search to create one of the most sophisticated discovery engines on the web.

At its core, Pinterest is built around the concept of the "Pin" — an image (or video) saved to a board with a link back to its source on the web. Users create thematic boards and populate them by pinning content they discover either on Pinterest itself or on external websites via the Pinterest browser extension. Each Pin carries metadata: the source URL, any text description, the board category, and crucially, the image itself which Pinterest analyses with computer vision to understand what it contains.

Pinterest's home feed is driven by a multi-tower recommendation model that evaluates pins along three dimensions: interest graph signals (what topics and boards a user has engaged with), social graph signals (what similar users are pinning), and contextual signals (time of day, device, recent search activity). The system uses Pinterest's proprietary PinSage algorithm — a graph convolutional neural network trained on Pinterest's 3-billion-node pin graph — to produce embeddings that capture both visual and semantic similarity between images.

One of Pinterest's most impressive technical features is visual search, which allows users to select any region of an image and search for visually similar content. If you see a chair in a room photo and want to find just that chair, you can draw a box around it and Pinterest will return visually similar chairs. This capability, powered by deep convolutional neural networks, has evolved into Pinterest Lens — a mobile camera search tool that identifies objects, plants, fashion items, and food in real-world photos and finds related pins instantly.

💰How Does Pinterest Make Money?

Pinterest's entire revenue model rests on a single insight: its users arrive with buying intent, making ads here far more effective than on entertainment-first platforms.

Pinterest's revenue comes almost entirely from advertising, specifically "Promoted Pins" — ads that are designed to look and feel exactly like organic pins in the feed. This native ad format works because Pinterest users are actively in a planning mindset: they are searching for a wedding venue, planning a kitchen renovation, or building a wishlist. Research consistently shows that Pinterest ads generate significantly higher purchase intent than ads on other social platforms, with studies indicating that 83% of weekly Pinterest users have made a purchase based on content they saw on the platform.

The advertising products include Standard Promoted Pins (image ads in the feed), Video Promoted Pins, Shopping Ads (product catalogue ads linked directly to retailer inventory), and Collections Ads (a primary image with multiple product images below). Shopping Ads are increasingly important — Pinterest has invested heavily in making it possible for retailers to upload their full product catalogues and have those products automatically matched to relevant searches and boards, turning Pinterest into a visual shopping discovery engine.

Pinterest's international growth is a key strategic priority. While U.S. users generate approximately 80% of revenue despite representing only about 20% of global monthly active users, international users represent enormous monetisation upside. The company has been investing in ad infrastructure, local sales teams, and creator partnerships in Europe, Brazil, and Southeast Asia to close this gap. Pinterest has also launched direct links for advertisers and a Performance+ AI ad buying system that automates targeting and bidding to improve advertiser return on investment.

🎯What Makes Pinterest Different?

Pinterest occupies a category of one — understanding what sets it apart explains both its loyal user base and its advertising strength.

🧠

Future Intent, Not Past Behaviour

People pin things they want to do, buy, or become. Unlike social feeds that reflect your past, Pinterest is fundamentally about aspiration — making it uniquely valuable to advertisers.

👁️

Visual Search at Scale

Pinterest Lens can identify objects in real-world photos and find visually similar content. You can photograph a piece of furniture in a showroom and find it or something similar online instantly.

🛒

High Purchase Intent

83% of weekly users have made a purchase based on Pinterest content — a conversion rate that makes it among the most commercially effective discovery platforms available to brands.

⏰

Evergreen Content

Unlike Instagram or TikTok posts that fade in hours, Pins can drive traffic for years. A recipe pinned in 2015 may still receive thousands of clicks in 2025 — a fundamentally different content economy.

🌐

Traffic Driver

Pinterest sends more referral traffic to external websites than almost any other platform. Creators, bloggers, and publishers treat it as a long-term traffic channel rather than a short-term engagement metric.

🎨

Positive Platform Culture

Pinterest consistently ranks as one of the most positively perceived social platforms. Its focus on aspiration and creativity rather than social comparison results in lower user anxiety and higher satisfaction scores.

📅Pinterest's History

From a niche invitation-only launch to half a billion monthly users — Pinterest's growth story.

2010 — Launched by Invitation Only

Ben Silbermann, Paul Sciarra, and Evan Sharp launch Pinterest as an invite-only beta. Initial users are niche hobbyists and craft enthusiasts.

2012 — Explosive Growth

Pinterest reaches 10 million users faster than any website in history. Becomes one of the top referral traffic sources on the internet.

2014 — Promoted Pins Launch

First advertising products go live, marking the start of Pinterest's commercial monetisation. Advertisers immediately respond to its high-purchase-intent audience.

2017 — Pinterest Lens

Visual search via smartphone camera launches, allowing users to identify and search for real-world objects. One of the most sophisticated visual AI features on any consumer platform.

2019 — IPO on NYSE

Lists at $19/share with a $10B valuation. The IPO is one of the year's most anticipated tech listings.

2022 — Bill Ready Becomes CEO

Former Google Commerce president replaces co-founder Ben Silbermann, signalling an intensified focus on shopping and advertiser performance.

2024 — 553M Monthly Users, AI Shopping

Reaches record user numbers; launches Performance+ AI ad platform and deep integration with Amazon for shopping ads, unlocking significant new revenue potential.
