✈️What is Skyscanner?

Skyscanner is a global travel search platform used by over 100 million monthly users to compare flights, hotels, and car rentals from hundreds of providers worldwide.

Explain it like I'm 10

Skyscanner is a search engine for flights and travel. You tell it where you want to go and it searches all the airlines and travel websites to show you the cheapest options all in one place.

One really cool thing is the Everywhere search — you can search for flights from your airport to anywhere in the world, sorted by cheapest price. It is great when you don't know where to go but want to travel!

Founded in 2001 in Edinburgh, Scotland by Gareth Williams, Barry Smith, and Bonamy Finch, Skyscanner started as a hobby project comparing European flight prices before growing into one of the world's leading travel search platforms. The company was acquired by Chinese travel company Ctrip (now Trip.com Group) in 2016 for $1.74 billion.

Skyscanner operates across flights, hotels, and car rentals with a particularly strong reputation for flight search. The platform is known for its clean interface, comprehensive coverage of global airlines including low-cost carriers often excluded from traditional GDS systems, and innovative features like Everywhere search.

As part of Trip.com Group (formerly Ctrip), Skyscanner has access to Chinese travel demand and inventory — a significant advantage in covering Asia-Pacific routes and connecting Western travelers with Chinese carriers. The parent relationship has expanded Skyscanner's inventory depth particularly in Asian markets.

⚙️Innovative Travel Search Features

Skyscanner has developed distinctive features that differentiate it from competitors.

Skyscanner's Everywhere feature allows users to search for flights from any origin to all destinations simultaneously, displaying results on a map sorted by price. This open-destination search is uniquely powerful for flexible travelers and has driven significant organic growth through word-of-mouth among travel enthusiasts.

Whole Month search displays the cheapest days to fly within a calendar month — allowing price-flexible travelers to optimize their departure date without searching day by day. This feature is particularly popular with remote workers and students who have schedule flexibility.

Skyscanner's Price Alerts and flexible date matrix help travelers find optimal booking windows. The platform's machine learning models, trained on billions of historical searches, provide price prediction insights that guide booking timing decisions.

💰Meta-Search Business Model

Skyscanner's revenue comes from referrals to airlines and travel partners.

Like Kayak, Skyscanner earns through CPC and CPA commissions when users click through to book with airlines, hotels, or OTAs. The platform does not take payment for most bookings — users complete transactions on the provider's website after clicking from Skyscanner.

Direct booking on Skyscanner, where available, allows some partners to let users complete bookings without leaving the Skyscanner interface. This improves conversion rates and provides Skyscanner with richer booking data while generating higher-value CPA fees.

Skyscanner's B2B API business, Skyscanner for Business, provides flight search technology to other companies including airlines, OTAs, and corporate travel tools. This white-label search product generates licensing revenue and extends Skyscanner's technology into partner ecosystems.

📅History & Timeline

Skyscanner's journey from Edinburgh startup to global travel platform:

2001 — Founded in Edinburgh

Gareth Williams, Barry Smith, and Bonamy Finch build Skyscanner as a hobby project comparing European flight prices.

2007 — Full-Time Business

Skyscanner transitions from side project to full-time business as traffic and revenue grow to justify the commitment.

2012 — Mobile Apps Launch

Skyscanner launches highly-rated iOS and Android apps, capturing the growing mobile travel search market.

2016 — Ctrip Acquisition

Chinese travel company Ctrip (now Trip.com Group) acquires Skyscanner for $1.74 billion, expanding its global reach.

2023 — AI Travel Planning
