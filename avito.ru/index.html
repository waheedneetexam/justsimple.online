🛒What is Avito?

Avito is Russia's largest online classifieds marketplace, connecting millions of buyers and sellers across real estate, automobiles, electronics, jobs, and personal services.

Explain it like I'm 10

Avito is like Craigslist or eBay for Russia. People post ads to sell things they don't need — a used car, their apartment, old furniture — and other people find and buy them.

It is the biggest website in Russia for buying and selling things between regular people, with millions of listings across the whole country.

Founded in 2007 in Moscow by Jonas Nordlander and Filip Engelbert, Avito was acquired by Naspers (now Prosus) in 2013. The platform quickly dominated Russian classifieds by offering a superior user experience compared to earlier players like Irr.ru and Slando.

Avito operates across multiple categories including real estate, automobiles, electronics, fashion, home goods, pets, jobs, and professional services. The real estate and automotive categories are particularly dominant — Avito is the primary discovery platform for used car buyers in Russia and a major channel for rental and property listings.

As Russia's internet market became more isolated following geopolitical events, Avito's domestic dominance strengthened. The platform serves over 100 million monthly users and hosts hundreds of millions of active listings, making it one of the world's largest classifieds operations by any measure.

⚙️Key Categories and Verticals

Avito's multi-category approach covers the full spectrum of peer-to-peer commerce.

Avito Real Estate (Avito Nedvizhimost) is a leading platform for property rentals and sales in Russia. Both private sellers and professional real estate agents list properties, making it a comprehensive destination for housing searches comparable to Zillow in the US market.

Avito Auto (Avito Avto) is Russia's largest used car marketplace. Private sellers and dealerships list millions of vehicles, and the platform has added valuation tools, history reports, and financing services to become a more complete automotive transaction platform.

Avito Services connects clients with freelancers and professional service providers across categories including home repair, beauty, tutoring, legal, and healthcare. This expanding services marketplace mirrors the evolution seen in other classifieds platforms that have extended beyond physical goods.

💰Monetization Strategy

Avito has evolved from free classifieds to a multifaceted marketplace business.

Basic listing is free for private sellers, but Avito charges for premium placement, promoted listings, and extended features. Professional sellers — dealers, agencies, and businesses — pay subscription fees for access to bulk listing tools and analytics.

Avito Delivery, launched to support safe P2P transactions, enables buyers and sellers to transact without meeting in person. This escrow-protected delivery service generates revenue from logistics partnerships and transaction fees, while increasing user trust and platform stickiness.

Value-added services including vehicle history reports, property valuations, and credit checks in partnership with financial institutions provide meaningful incremental revenue and improve the transaction experience — encouraging more high-value deals to complete on the platform.

📅History & Timeline

Avito's growth to Russian classifieds dominance:

2007 — Founded

Jonas Nordlander and Filip Engelbert launch Avito in Moscow, targeting the underserved Russian online classifieds market.

2013 — Naspers Acquisition

South African media giant Naspers acquires Avito in a deal that values the company at over $1 billion.

2015 — Market Leader

Avito surpasses OLX and all Russian competitors to become the undisputed leader in Russian online classifieds.

2020 — Avito Delivery Launch

Avito Delivery launches nationwide, enabling safe P2P transactions by mail and increasing transaction completion rates.

2022 — Domestic Dominance

Avito strengthens its position as international companies exit Russia, becoming an even more central marketplace in Russian e-commerce.
