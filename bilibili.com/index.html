🎬What is Bilibili?

Part YouTube, part Twitch, part anime streaming service, part social network — Bilibili defies easy categorisation but dominates Chinese youth culture.

Explain it like I'm 10

Imagine YouTube, but while you watch a video, hundreds of other people's comments float across the screen in real time — jokes, reactions, corrections — like watching with a crowd. That's Bilibili's famous "bullet comment" (danmaku) system and it completely changes how watching videos feels. Instead of lonely scrolling, it feels like a shared experience even if the video is five years old.

Bilibili started as a website for anime fans to watch shows and share reactions, but it grew into a massive platform covering everything: science lectures, cooking tutorials, gaming streams, music, beauty, and more. It's especially popular with Chinese people under 35 who grew up with the internet and want entertainment that feels genuinely created for them, not just imported from TV.

Bilibili Inc. (哔哩哔哩, nicknamed B站) is a Chinese online entertainment platform listed on both the NASDAQ and Hong Kong Stock Exchange. Founded in June 2009 by Xu Yi (known online as Xu Yi), it began as a community site for fans of the anime series "Magical Girl Madoka Magica" before rapidly expanding into a full-spectrum video and live-streaming platform. The name "Bilibili" derives from a character in the anime series Toaru Kagaku no Railgun, reflecting its deep roots in ACG (Anime, Comics, Games) culture.

What made Bilibili unusual was its unusually strict community gatekeeping in its early years. New users had to pass a 100-question exam about ACG culture before gaining full membership — a deliberate friction that ensured the early community was composed of genuine enthusiasts rather than casual visitors. This created a highly engaged, knowledgeable user base with strong community identity. While the membership exam has since been relaxed, the community ethos of shared cultural knowledge and respectful engagement remains a distinguishing feature.

Today Bilibili has 336 million monthly active users and serves as the primary video consumption platform for Chinese Gen-Z. Its content spans official anime licences, user-generated videos (UGC), professionally generated content (PGC), live streaming, mobile games, and e-commerce. Unlike Western platforms that treat young users as a demographic to monetise, Bilibili positions itself as a community first — a philosophy that has earned fierce loyalty even as competition from Douyin (TikTok's Chinese version) and Kuaishou intensifies.

⚙️How Bilibili Works

Bilibili's technical architecture balances massive video delivery with its signature danmaku comment system — one of the most complex real-time annotation systems on the internet.

Bilibili's most distinctive technical feature is the danmaku (弹幕) bullet-comment system. When a user posts a comment, it is timestamped to a specific moment in the video. Every subsequent viewer who reaches that timestamp sees the comment float across their screen — creating the effect of a synchronised communal viewing experience regardless of when each viewer watches. Managing millions of timestamped comments per video, rendering them at correct positions, and filtering inappropriate content in real time requires sophisticated infrastructure that Bilibili has refined over 15 years.

The content recommendation system uses a multi-objective ranking model that optimises simultaneously for completion rate (did the user finish the video?), interaction rate (did they comment, like, or coin?), and sharing behaviour. Bilibili's unique "coin" metric — users can give videos up to two coins as a stronger endorsement than a simple like — gives the algorithm a higher-signal engagement indicator than most platforms. Videos that accumulate coins reliably indicate deep appreciation rather than passive consumption.

Bilibili operates a dual-channel distribution model: an official content channel for licenced anime and professionally produced series, and a UGC channel for the millions of creator videos uploaded daily. The platform uses a Content Quality Index (CQI) system to surface high-quality UGC alongside professional content, ensuring new creators can break through without needing existing audiences. Creators earn income through a combination of platform revenue sharing, fan subscriptions (charging fees for exclusive content), sponsored content, and Bilibili's own creator support funds.

💰How Does Bilibili Make Money?

Bilibili's monetisation strategy is deliberately diversified to reduce dependence on advertising — a strategic choice that reflects its community-first philosophy.

Bilibili's largest revenue segment is value-added services (VAS), which includes premium membership subscriptions (大会员, "Grand Member") that grant access to early anime episodes, exclusive content, and platform perks. This subscription model generates predictable recurring revenue and aligns incentives — Bilibili profits when users love the platform enough to pay for it, rather than when they click ads. Over 22 million users hold premium subscriptions, generating significant monthly recurring revenue.

Mobile gaming has historically been Bilibili's second-largest revenue stream. The platform operates as a games publisher and distributor, leveraging its massive anime and gaming community to launch mobile games based on popular IP. Its biggest title, Fate/Grand Order (distributed in China), generated enormous revenue. However, China's gaming licence restrictions have created volatility in this segment, pushing Bilibili to diversify towards advertising and e-commerce.

Advertising is the fastest-growing segment and includes display ads, brand partnerships, and native video ads integrated into creator content. Bilibili has been expanding its ad platform capabilities with better audience targeting and brand safety tools to attract major advertisers. E-commerce integration — allowing creators to link products directly in videos — represents the newest revenue frontier, following the success of similar features on Douyin.

🧩What Makes Bilibili Unique

Several features distinguish Bilibili from every other video platform and explain its extraordinary hold on Chinese Gen-Z.

💬

Danmaku Bullet Comments

Timestamped floating comments create a synchronised communal experience regardless of when you watch — turning every video into a shared event.

🎓

Knowledgeable Community

Bilibili hosts some of China's best science, history, and engineering content — creators like "回形针PaperClip" have produced documentary-quality explainers watched 100M+ times.

🪙

The Coin System

Users can give videos up to two virtual coins as a high-signal endorsement. Coins are a more meaningful metric than likes, rewarding genuinely valued content.

🎌

ACG Heritage

Deep roots in Anime, Comics, and Games culture give Bilibili a distinct identity. The community speaks a shared cultural language that creates belonging for young Chinese users.

🎮

Gaming Integration

The gaming tab within Bilibili is a major distribution platform for Chinese mobile games. Community enthusiasm around game launches drives massive organic marketing.

📺

Multi-Format Creator Economy

Creators can earn via ads, fan subscriptions, platform funds, merchandise, and live-stream gifts — one of the most complete creator income ecosystems in the world.

📅Bilibili History

From an anime fan site to China's Gen-Z cultural hub in 15 years.

2009 — Founded by Xu Yi

Launches as a video site for anime fans (ACG community), initially centred on the Magical Girl Madoka Magica fandom.

2012 — Danmaku Becomes Central

The bullet-comment feature matures into Bilibili's defining characteristic, creating a uniquely shared viewing experience.

2018 — NASDAQ IPO

Lists on NASDAQ at $11.22/share, raising $483M. The IPO brings international attention to China's Gen-Z entertainment market.

2019 — Anime & Content Expansion

Signs major anime licencing deals and launches a dedicated documentary channel, expanding far beyond gaming and ACG.

2021 — Hong Kong Secondary Listing

Lists on HKEX to broaden access for Chinese retail investors; reaches 272M monthly users.

2022 — Membership Exam Relaxed

Drops the strict ACG entrance exam to lower barriers for new users while maintaining community guidelines.

2024 — Path to Profitability

After years of losses, reaches near-breakeven through advertising growth, subscription expansion, and cost discipline.
