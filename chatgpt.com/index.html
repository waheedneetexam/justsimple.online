What is ChatGPT?

The AI assistant that made artificial intelligence accessible to everyone.

🤖 Imagine a computer that has read almost every book and website ever written...

Imagine if you read millions of books, articles, and websites, and then someone could ask you any question and you'd always have a thoughtful answer. That's kind of what ChatGPT is — except it's a computer program, not a person. It learned from a huge amount of text written by humans, and now it can write essays, answer questions, write computer code, tell stories, and have conversations — almost like a very knowledgeable assistant.

ChatGPT doesn't actually "know" things the way people do — it predicts what words should come next based on patterns it learned. But it's so good at this that the answers usually seem very intelligent. It's like a very sophisticated autocomplete that can write paragraphs instead of just words.

Simple version: ChatGPT is an AI chatbot made by a company called OpenAI. You type a question or request, and it writes back a helpful, detailed, and surprisingly human-sounding answer — instantly, on almost any topic.

⚙️How Does ChatGPT Actually Work?

The technology behind the magic — explained without a PhD.

ChatGPT is built on a Large Language Model (LLM) — specifically, OpenAI's GPT-4o. An LLM is a neural network trained on vast amounts of text: books, websites, academic papers, code repositories, and more. During training, the model learns statistical patterns — which words tend to follow which other words, how sentences are structured, what answers typically follow what questions. It doesn't "understand" in the human sense; it predicts.

But raw prediction isn't enough to make a helpful assistant. OpenAI used a technique called Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF): human trainers rated thousands of model outputs, and the model was adjusted to produce responses humans rated as more helpful, harmless, and honest. This fine-tuning is what turns a generic text predictor into something that feels genuinely useful and coherent.

When you send a message, it's broken into "tokens" (roughly pieces of words), processed through billions of parameters in the neural network, and the model generates a response token by token — each word informed by everything that came before it in the conversation. A context window (up to 128,000 tokens in GPT-4 Turbo) determines how much of the conversation the model can "see" at once.

💰How OpenAI Makes Money

From non-profit origins to a $157 billion valuation.

OpenAI was founded in 2015 as a non-profit research lab. By 2019 it had restructured into a "capped-profit" company to attract investment — with a cap on investor returns (originally 100x) to preserve the non-profit mission. Microsoft invested $1 billion in 2019, then $10 billion more in 2023, making it OpenAI's primary cloud partner and integrating ChatGPT into Bing, Office 365, and Azure.

Revenue comes from three main streams: ChatGPT Plus and Team subscriptions ($20–$30/month), enterprise contracts (ChatGPT Enterprise, used by 93% of Fortune 500 companies), and the API (developers paying per token to integrate GPT-4 into their own products). OpenAI's revenue grew from essentially zero in 2022 to $3.7 billion in 2024, with projections of $11.6 billion in 2025.

📅OpenAI's History

2015 — OpenAI Founded

Sam Altman, Elon Musk, Greg Brockman, Ilya Sutskever, and others co-found OpenAI as a non-profit in San Francisco. Elon Musk pledges $1 billion.

2018 — GPT-1 Released

OpenAI publishes GPT-1 (117M parameters) — demonstrating that large language models trained on internet text could generate coherent prose.

2019 — Capped-Profit & Microsoft

OpenAI restructures into a capped-profit entity. Microsoft invests $1 billion. Elon Musk departs the board (later sues OpenAI).

2020 — GPT-3: A Sensation

GPT-3 (175B parameters) is released via API. Its ability to write code, essays, and poetry stuns researchers. Waitlist has 300K+ people.

2022 — ChatGPT Launches

November 30, 2022: ChatGPT goes live. 1 million users in 5 days. 100 million in 2 months — the fastest-growing consumer application in history.

2023 — GPT-4 & $13B Microsoft

GPT-4 releases with multimodal capabilities. Microsoft invests $10 billion more. OpenAI briefly fires then reinstates CEO Sam Altman in a dramatic boardroom crisis.

2024 — GPT-4o & $157B Valuation

GPT-4o ("omni") launches with real-time voice, vision, and text. OpenAI raises $6.6 billion at a $157 billion valuation — one of the largest private funding rounds ever.

⚖️Controversies & Concerns

Transformative technology always comes with difficult questions.

ChatGPT's rapid rise sparked intense debates about job displacement, academic integrity, misinformation, and AI safety. Within months of launch, universities scrambled to update plagiarism policies; news organisations found AI-generated articles being submitted as original journalism; and studies showed AI could pass bar exams, medical licensing tests, and MBA finals. The economic implications are real — McKinsey estimates generative AI could automate 30% of working hours globally by 2030.

Training data is a persistent controversy. GPT models were trained on vast amounts of internet text without explicit permission from creators, leading to multiple lawsuits from authors (including John Grisham and George R.R. Martin) and news organisations (The New York Times). The legal question of whether training on copyrighted content constitutes infringement remains unresolved in most jurisdictions.

On AI safety, OpenAI's own mission is to ensure artificial general intelligence benefits all of humanity — yet the company races to release increasingly powerful models. Co-founder Ilya Sutskever departed in 2024 citing safety concerns. The tension between safety research and commercial pressure is openly acknowledged within the organisation, and regulators in the EU, UK, and US are all working on AI governance frameworks prompted largely by ChatGPT's breakout success.
