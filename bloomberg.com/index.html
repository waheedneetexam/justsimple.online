📈What is Bloomberg?

Bloomberg L.P. is a global financial services, software, and media company best known for the Bloomberg Terminal and its authoritative financial news coverage.

Explain it like I'm 10

Bloomberg is like a super-powered tool for people who work with money. It shows them stock prices, news about companies, and helps them make smart decisions — all in real time.

Bloomberg.com is the free website part where anyone can read business news. But professionals pay a lot of money for the full Bloomberg Terminal with all the data.

Founded in 1981 by Michael Bloomberg, the company started by building the Bloomberg Terminal — a specialized computer system providing real-time financial data, analytics, and news to Wall Street professionals. The Terminal revolutionized finance by making market data universally accessible to anyone who could afford the subscription.

Bloomberg Terminal subscriptions cost approximately $24,000 per user per year and serve over 325,000 subscribers at financial institutions, hedge funds, investment banks, and government agencies worldwide. Terminal revenues fund Bloomberg's extensive news operation, one of the largest in the world.

Bloomberg News, the company's media division, operates bloomberg.com and Bloomberg Television, employs over 2,700 journalists in more than 100 countries, and is widely regarded as the gold standard for financial journalism and breaking market news.

⚙️The Bloomberg Terminal

The Bloomberg Terminal is the foundation of Bloomberg's business and the most powerful financial data tool in the world.

The Terminal provides real-time and historical data on over 35 million financial instruments including equities, fixed income, foreign exchange, derivatives, and commodities. Users access this data through the Bloomberg keyboard's distinctive yellow keys and a command-line interface that has barely changed in decades.

Beyond data, the Terminal includes Bloomberg's news feed, research from thousands of investment banks, analytics tools for portfolio management, risk assessment, and deal analysis. It also includes Bloomberg Messaging (IB), a secure messaging system used widely across Wall Street.

The Terminal's interface is notoriously complex, requiring weeks of training to use effectively. This complexity is a feature, not a bug — it creates high switching costs that lock subscribers in and make Bloomberg difficult to displace despite its high price.

💰Bloomberg Media and Beyond

Bloomberg has expanded from the Terminal into a global media and data services empire.

Bloomberg Television broadcasts 24/7 financial news to over 300 million households worldwide. Bloomberg Radio, Bloomberg Businessweek magazine, and Bloomberg CityLab for urban affairs round out a comprehensive media portfolio targeting professional and executive audiences.

Bloomberg Government and Bloomberg Law are specialized data platforms serving government affairs professionals and legal practitioners respectively — extending the Terminal model to adjacent professional markets with sector-specific data and analysis.

Bloomberg Philanthropies, Michael Bloomberg's charitable organization, has become one of the largest in the world, donating billions to public health, climate change, education, and the arts — further enhancing the Bloomberg brand's global reputation.

📅History & Timeline

Bloomberg's evolution from startup to global institution:

1981 — Bloomberg Founded

Michael Bloomberg founds Innovative Market Systems (later Bloomberg L.P.) after being laid off from Salomon Brothers with a $10M severance.

1990 — Bloomberg News

Bloomberg News wire service launches, beginning the company's transformation from data provider to major news organization.

1994 — Bloomberg Television

Bloomberg Television launches as a 24-hour financial news channel, competing directly with CNBC.

2001 — Bloomberg Becomes Mayor

Michael Bloomberg is elected Mayor of New York City, stepping back from active management but retaining ownership.

2020 — Bloomberg Businessweek

Bloomberg continues expanding its media brand, with Businessweek, CityLab, and digital products reaching new audiences.
