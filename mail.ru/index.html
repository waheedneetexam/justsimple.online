Mail.ru: Russia's Email Giant and Internet Portal

An independent deep-dive into Mail.ru's history, its email infrastructure, portal services, and its evolution under VK Company as part of Russia's domestic internet ecosystem.

🌐 mail.ru

Editorial Independence: JustSimple.Online is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Mail.ru. All analysis is independent research for informational purposes only.

📧What is Mail.ru?

Mail.ru is Russia's answer to Gmail — the country's most-used email service, combined with a news portal, search, maps, and entertainment, all under one login.

Explain it like I'm 10

Mail.ru is like Gmail but made for Russian speakers. It's an email service that tens of millions of Russians use to send messages, but it also has a news homepage, weather forecasts, games, and a search engine. If you use it, you log in once and can see the news, check the weather, and read your email all in one place.

Mail.ru is now owned by the same company that owns VK (Russia's social network). They share a login system so your Mail.ru account can also work on VK and other Russian websites. Mail.ru was actually one of Russia's first internet companies and helped build what became the Russian internet — or 'Runet' as Russians call it.

Mail.ru was founded in 1998 during the early days of the Russian internet. The email service launched at a time when Russian-language alternatives to US email providers were nearly absent. Offering free email in Russian with generous storage and a familiar Cyrillic interface, Mail.ru rapidly became the dominant email service for the Russian-speaking internet. The service was developed by DataArt Software and later became the core product of Mail.ru Group, which became one of Russia's largest technology conglomerates.

Mail.ru Group grew into a diversified internet holding company, acquiring or founding social networks (VK, Odnoklassniki), games (My.Games), food delivery (Delivery Club, acquired and then sold), ride-hailing (Citymobil), and edtech (GeekBrains). In 2021, Mail.ru Group rebranded as VK Company to unify its brands under the VK identity. The Mail.ru email and portal services continued operating under their original branding as the authentication and communication backbone of VK Company's ecosystem.

Today, Mail.ru serves over 60 million active email users and remains Russia's second-most visited website after VK. The portal homepage aggregates news, weather, sports, and entertainment content with a personalised feed. Following Western sanctions and the departure of many international tech companies from Russia after 2022, Mail.ru's position strengthened as Russian internet users consolidated around domestic platforms. The unified VK ID login system connecting Mail.ru, VK, and other VK Company services creates a domestic single sign-on ecosystem.

⚙️How Mail.ru Works

Mail.ru's email infrastructure handles billions of messages monthly for Russia's largest email service.

Mail.ru's email infrastructure is built on a custom stack developed for Russian-language email at scale. The service handles billions of messages monthly, with aggressive spam filtering (Mail.ru has historically had sophisticated anti-spam technology given the high volume of Russian-language spam). The email client has evolved from a basic web interface to a full-featured webmail application with offline support, calendar integration, and cloud storage (Mail.ru Cloud — 8GB free). Mobile apps for iOS and Android provide synchronised access.

The Mail.ru portal aggregates content from across VK Company's properties and third-party Russian news sources. Personalisation algorithms surface news, sports, and entertainment based on reading history and explicit interests. The portal serves as an alternative to Yandex.ru as a homepage-style starting point for Russian internet sessions. The portal's high traffic makes it prime real estate for digital advertising — news articles, weather widgets, and the email inbox all carry targeted display advertising.

Mail.ru integrates with VK ID (VK Company's single sign-on) to provide unified authentication across VK, Mail.ru, OK.ru, and other VK Company services. This SSO system is strategically important: by creating a common identity layer, VK Company can track user behaviour across all its properties and build richer targeting profiles for advertisers. Mail.ru's cloud storage service integrates with VK's media ecosystem, allowing photo and video sharing between email and VK.

💰How Mail.ru Makes Money

Mail.ru's revenue comes from digital advertising on its portal and email, premium email subscriptions, and cloud storage.

Display advertising on the Mail.ru portal and within the email interface is the primary revenue source. The homepage portal attracts tens of millions of daily visitors who see targeted display ads; email advertising (sidebar ads and sponsored messages in the promotions tab) add to the inventory. Mail.ru's targeting uses the VK ID data ecosystem — knowing a user's VK profile, social connections, and browsing history allows sophisticated targeting beyond what anonymous portal visitors allow.

Mail.ru Cloud premium subscriptions offer additional storage beyond the free 8GB tier. Subscription tiers range from 64GB to 4TB, priced competitively with Google One and iCloud. The cloud storage business benefits from lock-in — users who store photos and documents in Mail.ru Cloud are more likely to remain Mail.ru email users. Integration with VK's photo storage ecosystem adds additional utility.

As part of VK Company, Mail.ru's revenue is reported within the broader VK Company financial results rather than separately. VK Company's total revenue (encompassing VK social network, Mail.ru, OK.ru, and My.Games) was estimated at approximately 100 billion rubles (~$1.1B at pre-2022 exchange rates) annually. Mail.ru's specific contribution includes premium email, cloud storage, and a significant share of the company's digital advertising revenue through its portal and email advertising inventory.

📅History & Timeline

From Russia's first major web email to the backbone of VK Company's digital ecosystem.

1998 — Mail.ru Launches

Launches as one of Russia's first free web-based email services during the early days of the Russian internet.

2001 — Mail.ru Group Formed

Mail.ru becomes the flagship product of a holding company that will grow to encompass social networks, games, and more.

2010 — Mail.ru Group IPO on London Stock Exchange

Raises $912M in an IPO, one of the largest Russian tech listings of its time.

2015 — Acquires Stake in Delivery Club

Mail.ru Group expands into food delivery and ride-hailing, becoming a diversified Russian internet conglomerate.

2021 — Rebrands as VK Company

Mail.ru Group renames to VK Company to unify under the strongest brand in its portfolio.

2022 — Consolidation After Western Exits

As Western tech companies restrict Russian access, Mail.ru and VK ecosystem gains users from departing platforms.
